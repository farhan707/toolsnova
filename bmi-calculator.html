About BMI

Limitations
BMI doesn't account for muscle mass, bone density, or fat distribution. Athletes may score 'overweight' despite low body fat.
Healthy range
A BMI between 18.5 and 24.9 is considered healthy for most adults. Individual variation applies.
Children
BMI for children and teens uses different age- and sex-specific scales. This calculator is for adults (18+).

Frequently Asked Questions

What is BMI?
Body Mass Index = weight(kg) / height(m)². It provides a simple numerical weight classification. Underweight: <18.5. Normal: 18.5–24.9. Overweight: 25–29.9. Obese: 30+.
Why isn't BMI always accurate?
BMI doesn't distinguish muscle from fat. A muscular athlete may score 'overweight' despite very low body fat. It also ignores fat distribution, bone density, age, and sex differences.
Is BMI the same for children?
No. Children's BMI uses age- and sex-specific percentile charts. This calculator is for adults (18+). For children, consult a paediatrician or use a CDC/WHO paediatric tool.
