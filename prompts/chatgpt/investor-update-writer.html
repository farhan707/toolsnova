📋 Prompt
You are a startup advisor who has reviewed 500+ investor updates and knows what builds investor confidence.

Company: [name and one-line description]
Period: [month/quarter, e.g. June 2026]
Metrics: [MRR, users, revenue, burn rate, runway]
Wins: [main wins]
Challenges: [main challenges — honest]
Ask: [specific ask or 'no ask this period']

Write a complete investor update — investors read in 90 seconds, structure for scanning:

SUBJECT: [Company] [Period] — [headline metric]
📊 METRICS: Table Metric | This period | Last period | Change
🚀 HIGHLIGHTS (3 bullets): Specific wins
⚠️ CHALLENGES (2 bullets): Honest — builds trust
🎯 NEXT PERIOD: Top 2–3 priorities
💰 FINANCIALS: Revenue/MRR, burn rate, runway (months)
🙏 ASK: One specific actionable request
PERSONAL NOTE: 2–3 sentences of founder's honest perspective

Tone: Transparent, confident, concise. No spin.

💡 Pro Tips
Send every 30 days — investors who hear from you regularly help proactively and reinvest more often
Lead with the metrics table — investors scan numbers first, narrative second
Never hide bad news — investors find out anyway; hidden problems permanently destroy trust
The Ask section is most underused — investors want to help but need a specific request
⚠️ Common Mistakes
Monthly updates becoming quarterly — inconsistency signals trouble
All wins no challenges — experienced investors know every business has problems
Vague metrics ('strong growth') — specific numbers only
No ask — the whole point of regular updates is staying connected and available to help
❓ FAQ
  • How often to send?
    Monthly for early-stage. Quarterly for later-stage. Never let more than 90 days pass.
  • Ideal length?
    90 seconds to read — roughly 300–400 words plus a metrics table.
  • Should I send bad news?
    Yes, immediately. Bad news investors discover from a third party is far more damaging than proactive disclosure.
  • Best model?
    Claude — maintains transparency and confidence without promotionalism, structures the 90-second-scan format naturally.
