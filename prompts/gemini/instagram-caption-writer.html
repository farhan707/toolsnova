📋 Prompt
You are an Instagram content strategist who writes captions that drive saves, shares, and meaningful comments.

Image: [image description]
Brand voice: [brand voice — inspirational/educational/humorous/professional/raw and honest]
Goal: [caption goal — engagement/traffic/awareness/sales]
Niche: [niche]

Task: Write 3 Instagram caption variations:

CAPTION 1 — Storytelling:
[Hook line — stops the scroll]
[2–3 short paragraphs telling a story connected to the image]
[Question to drive comments]
[Subtle CTA]
[Dots separator]
[20–30 hashtags: 5 large/10 medium/10 small]

CAPTION 2 — Educational:
[Bold statement or surprising fact]
[3–5 insight bullets with ✓ or → ]
['Save this for later' or 'Which tip are you trying first?']
[Hashtags]

CAPTION 3 — Short and punchy:
[1–2 powerful sentences]
[Single CTA]
[5–10 hashtags]

HASHTAG STRATEGY: Mix large/medium/small for reach + ranking opportunities.
📄 Example output
CAPTION 1 (Storytelling):
Six months ago I posted every single day and gained 200 followers.

Then I posted 3x a week with real intention, and gained 4,000.

The algorithm didn't change. I did.

I stopped making content to fill a schedule and started making content that actually had something to say. The first time I hit publish on something I was genuinely scared to share — it became my most saved post.

Consistency matters. But consistency of quality beats consistency of quantity, every time.

What's the piece of content you've been too scared to post? 👇

💡 Pro Tips
The first line determines whether the 'more' button gets clicked — make it a scroll-stopper
Captions that end with a question get 3–4x more comments than captions that don't
Line breaks are your friend on Instagram — no walls of text
Respond to every comment in the first 60 minutes after posting — it signals to the algorithm that your post is creating engagement
⚠️ Common Mistakes
Putting hashtags in the caption body (they distract from the text) — put them at the end after dots or in the first comment
Using only massive hashtags (#love, #photography) — you'll never rank in those; mix in niche hashtags where you can compete
Generic hooks like 'Today I want to share...' — the algorithm shows 2 lines; make them count
Ignoring the image's emotional context — the best captions extend or contrast the emotion in the image
❓ FAQ
  • How many hashtags should I use?
    Instagram's own guidance suggests 3–5 highly relevant hashtags in 2026. Many creators see results with 10–20 mixed hashtags. Test both approaches and track which drives more reach for your specific account.
  • Should I put hashtags in the caption or first comment?
    Either works algorithmically in 2026. First comment keeps captions cleaner. Caption placement gets hashtags indexed faster. Try both and see what your audience responds to.
  • Can I use the same captions repeatedly?
    The story and educational format captions should be unique each time. However, you can adapt a high-performing caption structure to different topics — it's the format, not the content, that you're repeating.
  • How important is the posting time on Instagram?
    Posting when your audience is most active helps initial engagement velocity, which affects algorithm reach. Check your Instagram Insights for your specific audience's active hours — general advice (post at 9am Tuesdays) is rarely as accurate as your own data.
