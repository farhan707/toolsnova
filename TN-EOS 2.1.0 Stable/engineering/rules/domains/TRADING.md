# Trading Domain

## Purpose

Ensure trading tools promote informed decision-making through accurate calculations, transparent methodology, and responsible risk management.

Trading tools support analysis.

They do not predict markets.

---

# Core Principles

Every trading calculator must:

- Produce deterministic calculations.
- Explain assumptions.
- Define every variable.
- Avoid predictive language.
- Encourage responsible risk management.

Trading calculators educate.

They never promise outcomes.

---

# Trading Concepts

Explain terminology whenever used.

Examples:

- Margin
- Leverage
- Pip
- Lot Size
- Drawdown
- Risk Reward
- Swap
- Spread
- Equity
- Free Margin

Never assume users understand trading jargon.

---

# Risk Management

Whenever applicable explain:

✓ Position sizing

✓ Capital preservation

✓ Risk percentage

✓ Leverage implications

✓ Margin requirements

Trading tools should educate rather than encourage excessive risk.

---

# Formula Transparency

Every calculation should include:

✓ Formula

✓ Variables

✓ Units

✓ Assumptions

✓ Interpretation

Users should understand how results are produced.

---

# Practical Examples

Use realistic market scenarios.

Avoid unrealistic account balances, leverage, or trade sizes.

Examples should:

✓ Match calculator behavior

✓ Reflect realistic trading conditions

✓ Help users understand the result

---

# Educational Value

Users should understand:

- What is being calculated
- Why it matters
- How to use the result
- Common mistakes
- Important assumptions

Education builds confidence and trust.

---

# Result Interpretation

Whenever a trading calculator produces a result, explain what it means in practical trading terms.

Interpretation should help users understand:

- What the result represents.
- Whether the result indicates higher or lower risk.
- What assumptions influence the outcome.
- When caution is appropriate.

Interpretations should remain educational.

They must never predict future market direction.

---

# Decision Support

Whenever appropriate, provide practical guidance based on the calculated result.

Examples include:

- Position sizing considerations.
- Risk management suggestions.
- Capital preservation guidance.
- Trade planning recommendations.

Decision support should encourage disciplined trading rather than aggressive trading.

---

# Trading Workflow

Trading calculators should support complete trading workflows.

Whenever practical, guide users toward the logical next calculator rather than unrelated tools.

Examples:

Market Analysis

↓

Entry Planning

↓

Risk Management

↓

Position Sizing

↓

Trade Evaluation

Workflow guidance should improve task completion rather than page navigation.

---

# Business Validation

Mathematically correct results may still be impractical.

Trading calculators should identify unrealistic or unsafe scenarios whenever appropriate.

Examples include:

- Extremely high leverage
- Near-zero stop-loss distance
- Unrealistic account growth
- Impossible position sizes
- Excessive capital risk

Business validation protects users beyond mathematical correctness.

---

# Neutrality

Never imply:

- Guaranteed profit
- Winning strategies
- Market predictions
- Risk-free trading

Maintain educational neutrality.

---

# Trust and Transparency

Whenever practical, trading pages should include:

✓ Formula

✓ Worked example

✓ Assumptions

✓ Limitations

✓ References

✓ Last updated information

Users should understand both the calculation and its limitations.

---

# References

Prefer authoritative sources.

Examples:

- Broker documentation
- Exchange specifications
- Regulatory publications
- Platform documentation

Whenever possible, prefer primary sources over secondary summaries.

---

# Completion

Trading Domain requirements are satisfied when users can:

✓ Understand the calculation

✓ Verify the methodology

✓ Interpret the result

✓ Apply the result responsibly

✓ Recognize trading risks

✓ Understand assumptions

✓ Follow the next logical trading step
