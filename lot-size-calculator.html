About lot size calculation

Risk management
Never risk more than 1–2% per trade. This calculator ensures your position size matches your risk tolerance.
Pip value
For standard EUR/USD lots, pip value is ~$10. For gold (XAUUSD), use $1 per 0.01 move = $100/lot for $1 move.
Formula
Lot size = Risk Amount / (Stop Loss in pips × Pip Value per lot)

How to Use the Lot Size Calculator

  1. Enter your account balance — your total equity including open positions.
  2. Set risk percentage — the percentage of account to risk on this trade. Use 0.5–1% for prop firm accounts, 1–2% for personal accounts.
  3. Enter stop loss in pips — the distance from your entry price to your stop loss. This is determined by your chart analysis, not by your desired lot size.
  4. Select currency pair — choose your instrument. The calculator uses the correct pip value for each pair automatically.
  5. Read your lot size — the exact position size that risks the percentage you specified. Use this number precisely in your broker's order entry.
🔑 Golden rule: Always calculate lot size AFTER placing your stop loss on the chart. Your stop loss location is determined by market structure — support, resistance, swing highs/lows. Your lot size is then calculated to risk exactly your chosen percentage at that stop distance.

Understanding Lot Sizes

📦 Standard Lot
100,000 units of base currency. On EURUSD, 1 standard lot = $10 per pip. This is the largest standard position size — used by institutional traders and well-funded retail accounts.
📦 Mini Lot
10,000 units = 0.1 lots. $1 per pip on EURUSD. Suitable for accounts of $1,000–$10,000 with proper risk management.
📦 Micro Lot
1,000 units = 0.01 lots. $0.10 per pip on EURUSD. Used for very small accounts or for fine-tuning position sizes to the exact cent of risk.
💰 Pip Value
The dollar value of a 1-pip move per lot. Varies by pair and account currency. EURUSD: $10/pip/lot. USDJPY: ~$9.10/pip/lot. XAUUSD (Gold): $10/pip/lot but pips are $0.01 price moves.
📐 The Formula
Lot Size = (Account × Risk%) ÷ (Stop Loss Pips × Pip Value Per Lot). Example: $10,000 × 1% ÷ (20 pips × $10) = $100 ÷ $200 = 0.5 lots.
⚠️ Prop Firm Context
On a $100K prop firm account with 5% daily DD and 1% risk per trade, you can sustain 5 consecutive losing trades before breaching the daily limit. This is why 0.5% risk is recommended — giving 10 losing trades of buffer.

Lot Size and Risk Management

Why lot size is your most important decision

Every other aspect of trading — entry signals, indicators, news analysis — is secondary to position sizing. A trader with a mediocre strategy and perfect position sizing will outperform a trader with an excellent strategy and poor position sizing over time. Lot size directly controls your survival during losing streaks.

Adjusting for volatility

During high-volatility periods (major news events, market open/close), consider reducing your normal lot size by 30–50%. Wider spreads, slippage, and unpredictable price action mean your actual risk can be significantly higher than your calculated risk. Consistent traders reduce size in uncertain conditions.

Scaling into positions

Some traders split their intended position into 2–3 entries, adding to the position as it moves in their direction. If your normal size is 1 lot, enter 0.5 lots at the initial signal and add 0.5 lots after confirmation. This reduces the risk of full-size entry at a poor price while maintaining upside participation.

📊 Simple sizing rule for beginners: Use 1% risk per trade until you have 100 live trades recorded. After 100 trades, calculate your actual win rate and expectancy. Only then consider adjusting risk percentage based on your proven edge.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a lot in forex?
1 standard lot = 100,000 units of base currency. 1 mini lot = 10,000 units. 1 micro lot = 1,000 units. Most retail brokers allow trading in 0.01 lot increments.
Why is position sizing critical?
Position sizing determines how much you lose on a bad trade. Risking 1% per trade means even 50 consecutive losses only reduces the account by ~40%. Risking 5% makes 20 losses catastrophic.
What is the 1% risk rule?
Risk no more than 1–2% of account balance per trade. On a $10,000 account, 1% = $100 maximum loss. This ensures a long losing streak doesn't wipe the account.
