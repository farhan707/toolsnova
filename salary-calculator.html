How to Use the Salary Calculator

  1. Enter gross salary — your total salary before any deductions. This is the CTC (Cost to Company) or the figure on your offer letter.
  2. Select pay period — annual, monthly, or hourly. The calculator converts between all periods automatically.
  3. Enter tax information — your applicable tax rate or tax bracket. The calculator shows take-home pay after income tax.
  4. Add deductions — provident fund, health insurance, professional tax, and other standard deductions that reduce your taxable income.
  5. Review take-home pay — see net salary after all deductions, monthly take-home, and the effective tax rate on your income.
💼 CTC vs Take-Home: In India, CTC (Cost to Company) includes components that are not cash payments — employer's PF contribution, gratuity provision, and insurance premiums. Your actual take-home is typically 65–75% of CTC. Always ask for the in-hand salary breakdown before accepting an offer.

Understanding Salary Structure

💰 Basic Salary
The fixed core component — typically 40–50% of CTC. Forms the basis for calculating PF contribution, gratuity, and HRA. Higher basic means higher PF deduction but also higher retirement benefit.
🏠 HRA (House Rent Allowance)
Allowance for accommodation expenses. Tax-exempt up to the least of: actual HRA received, 50% of basic salary (metro) or 40% (non-metro), or actual rent paid minus 10% of basic. Significant tax saving for those paying rent.
🚗 Allowances
Transport, medical, leave travel, and special allowances form the variable component. Some are partially or fully tax-exempt up to specified limits. Structuring salary with higher allowances reduces tax liability.
🏦 Provident Fund (PF)
12% of basic salary contributed by employee + 12% matched by employer. Employee contribution is tax-deductible under Section 80C. Employer contribution is part of CTC but not take-home. PF accumulates at 8.1% p.a. — a guaranteed return.
📊 Income Tax Slabs
India (new regime 2024-25): Up to ₹3L: 0%. ₹3–7L: 5%. ₹7–10L: 10%. ₹10–12L: 15%. ₹12–15L: 20%. Above ₹15L: 30%. Standard deduction of ₹75,000 available under new regime.
🎁 Gratuity
5/26 × basic salary × years of service. Payable after 5 years of employment. Employer provisions gratuity as part of CTC but you only receive it upon leaving. Tax-exempt up to ₹20 lakhs.

Maximising Your Take-Home Salary

Salary restructuring for tax efficiency

The same CTC can produce very different take-home amounts depending on how it is structured. Components like HRA, LTA, food allowance, and telephone reimbursement are partially tax-exempt. Working with your employer's HR to maximise these components within your CTC can increase monthly take-home by 5–15% without any increase in CTC.

80C deductions — the tax saving toolkit

Under the old tax regime, Section 80C allows up to ₹1.5 lakh deduction from taxable income. Eligible investments: PPF, ELSS mutual funds, NSC, tax-saving FD, life insurance premiums, children's tuition fees, and home loan principal repayment. At 30% tax bracket, maximising 80C saves ₹45,000 in tax annually.

Old vs new tax regime

The new tax regime offers lower rates but eliminates most deductions. The old regime allows HRA, 80C, 80D (medical insurance), home loan interest, and other deductions. Generally: if total deductions exceed ₹3–3.5 lakhs, the old regime saves more tax. Use this calculator to model both and choose the more beneficial option.

💡 Negotiation tip: When negotiating salary, ask for the full CTC breakdown including all components before comparing offers. A higher CTC with poor structure (no HRA, all taxable) can result in lower take-home than a lower CTC with efficient structure. Focus on in-hand salary and benefits, not just the headline CTC number.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I convert hourly to annual salary?
Multiply your hourly rate by the number of working hours per year. Assuming 40 hours/week × 52 weeks = 2,080 working hours. So $25/hour × 2,080 = $52,000/year.
How do I calculate monthly salary from annual?
Divide annual salary by 12. Example: $60,000/year ÷ 12 = $5,000/month. For weekly: divide by 52. For daily: divide by 260 (52 weeks × 5 working days).
What is CTC vs take-home salary?
CTC (Cost to Company) includes everything the employer pays: base salary, allowances, employer's PF contribution, gratuity, and benefits. Take-home (in-hand) salary is what you actually receive after deducting income tax, PF, and other deductions — typically 70–80% of CTC.
How is income tax calculated on salary?
Income tax is calculated on taxable income (salary minus exemptions and deductions). Tax slabs vary by country. In India, the new regime starts taxing at 5% above ₹3 lakh. The tax estimate shown here is illustrative — consult a CA for accurate calculations.
