💡 Pro Tips
The easiest links you'll ever get are from resources pages — Google '[your niche] resources' and email every page that lists tools
Data-backed content earns links on autopilot — if you have original data (user numbers, tool usage stats), publish it and journalists find it
Never buy links — a single manual penalty from Google can set you back 12–18 months
Guest posting works but is increasingly devalued — only target publications your actual audience reads, with a DR above 50
⚠️ Common Mistakes
Chasing quantity over quality — 100 DR20 links is often worth less than 3 DR80 links for ranking
Sending the same outreach email to everyone — 1% reply rate on generic outreach vs. 20%+ on personalised outreach
Building links to your homepage only — distribute links to important inner pages too
Ignoring internal links — every new external link gets amplified if there's a clear internal link path to important pages
❓ FAQ
  • How many backlinks do I need to rank?
    It depends entirely on your niche and specific keyword competitiveness. For low-competition long-tail keywords, you can rank with zero backlinks. For competitive terms, check what the top 3 results have — that's your target zone.
  • Is guest posting still worth it in 2026?
    Guest posting on high-quality, editorially relevant sites still earns real SEO value. Mass guest posting on low-quality sites (often sold as 'DA 30+ guaranteed') is widely discounted by Google's algorithm.
  • Should I buy links?
    No. Google's manual review team specifically targets paid links, and the consequences of a manual penalty (which removes rankings, sometimes permanently) far outweigh the short-term ranking gains. Build links through content, tools, and genuine outreach.
  • What's the fastest legitimate link building tactic?
    For new sites, resource page link building and GitHub awesome-list submissions get the fastest results with the lowest effort. Digital PR (getting coverage from journalists) gets the highest-quality links but requires more upfront investment in content or data.
