📋 Prompt
You are a webinar specialist who scripts events converting 20%+ of attendees to trials or purchases.

Topic: [webinar topic]
Audience: [target audience — role, experience level]
Duration: [duration] minutes
CTA: [call to action — trial/demo/purchase/consult]

Task: Write a complete webinar script with timing:

[0–5 min] WELCOME: Introduce, housekeeping, agenda, 'stay to the end' hook
[5–15 min] THE PROBLEM: Establish pain with audience data + live poll
[15–45 min] THE VALUE: Framework/content — 80% genuine education, 20% product context
[45–55 min] PROOF: Demo or case study
[55–60 min] Q&A BRIDGE: Handle top 5 questions, bridge to offer
[60–70 min] THE PITCH: Soft offer, what's included, urgency if genuine
[Final] CTA: Clear action + bonus if applicable

Include: Poll questions, interaction prompts, technical notes for each section
📄 Example output
WEBINAR: 'How to Get 10× Better AI Output in 30 Minutes' | 60 min

[0–5 min — WELCOME]
Say: 'Welcome — dropping in the chat: what AI tool do you use most? ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini?'
[Tech note: Read 5–8 responses aloud to build community]
Say: 'Quick note: stay until the end — I have something that's not on the registration page.'

[5–15 min — THE PROBLEM]
POLL: 'Rate your satisfaction with your current AI output from 1–5. Type your number in the chat.'
[Read results] 'Most of you are 3–4. That's exactly where I was. Decent output, knowing it should be better, not knowing what to change. Here's why.'

[HOOK for retention]: 'In about 20 minutes I'm going to show you something that changes this permanently. Stick with me.'

[15–45 min — THE VALUE]
Say: 'The entire difference between average and excellent AI output comes down to prompt structure. Let me show you the framework...'
[Tech note: Screen share begins here]

[60 min — CTA]
Say: 'I promised something at the end. Here it is: toolsnova.net/ai-prompt-library — 200+ tested prompts, free, no signup required. For webinar attendees specifically, here's a link for a free account that saves your favourites: [link]. This expires in 48 hours.'
[Pin the link in chat immediately]

💡 Pro Tips
The 'stay to the end' promise at the start is your retention mechanism — make what you offer worth waiting for
Polls every 10–15 minutes break passive listening and create genuine engagement signals
The transition to the pitch should feel earned — build it naturally as the next logical step
80/20 rule is not optional: audiences who feel sold to disengage fast and don't convert
⚠️ Common Mistakes
Starting with 10 minutes of company history — attention is highest in minutes 1–5
Live demos that risk going wrong — pre-record your demo and play the recording
No urgency in the CTA — 'sign up any time' removes the impulse to act now
Reading directly from slides — slides should be visual prompts, not your full script
❓ FAQ
  • What platform should I use for webinars?
    Up to 100 attendees: Zoom or Google Meet. 100–1,000: Demio, Livestorm, or Webinar.com (better analytics and polls). Large scale: GoToWebinar or BigMarker. Record every webinar — the replay generates significant additional conversions.
  • What's a good webinar conversion rate?
    Industry averages: 20–40% of registrants attend live, 10–20% of attendees convert to the offer. Above 15% live-attendee conversion is excellent. Quality of audience (how warm they are) matters more than any single webinar tactic.
  • Live vs. evergreen webinars?
    Live creates genuine urgency and interaction. Evergreen scales 24/7 automatically. The optimal funnel: run live first to refine the content, then convert the best-performing version to evergreen. Always send the replay to registrants who didn't attend live.
  • How long should the pitch portion be?
    Under 15% of total length — 9 minutes for a 60-minute webinar. Long pitches cause drop-off and brand damage. The most effective pitches are concise, specific, and tied directly to the problem just discussed.
