📋 Prompt
You are a professional AI art director specialising in landscape and environmental Midjourney prompts.

Environment type: [environment type — forest/ocean/desert/urban/mountain/fantasy/sci-fi/interior]
Mood: [mood — serene/dramatic/mysterious/epic/intimate/desolate]
Time of day: [time of day — golden hour/blue hour/midday/night/overcast/storm]
Style: [style — photorealistic/painterly/cinematic/editorial/concept art/watercolour]

Task: Write 4 Midjourney landscape prompts:

FORMAT:
/imagine prompt: [detailed description], [atmosphere/weather], [lighting], [time of day], [photographic reference — camera/lens], [artistic style reference], [quality tags] --ar [ratio] --v 6.1 --stylize [value]

VARIATION 1 — Photorealistic nature photography
--ar 16:9, --stylize 150

VARIATION 2 — Cinematic/film still
--ar 2.39:1 (anamorphic), --stylize 250

VARIATION 3 — Epic concept art
--ar 16:9, --stylize 600

VARIATION 4 — Intimate editorial
--ar 4:5, --stylize 200

For each: specify the --no prompt (what to exclude) and explain what --stylize value does for this specific style
📄 Example output
MIDJOURNEY LANDSCAPE PROMPTS: Mountain Forest, Blue Hour, Serene/Mysterious

VARIATION 1 — PHOTOREALISTIC NATURE:
/imagine prompt: ancient pine forest in mountain highlands, blue hour twilight, soft violet and deep teal sky visible through the canopy, ground mist rising from a forest stream, scattered rocks covered in emerald moss, atmospheric perspective creating depth into the forest, shot on Sony A7R IV with 24mm f/2.8 lens, long exposure 30 seconds, tack-sharp foreground, creamy bokeh background, National Geographic photography style, natural light only --ar 16:9 --v 6.1 --stylize 150 --no artificial lighting, people, animals, text, watermark

[Stylize 150: Follows your description closely with Midjourney's natural photography aesthetic. Minimal artistic interpretation.]

VARIATION 2 — CINEMATIC:
/imagine prompt: cinematic wide shot of primordial mountain forest at blue hour, thick atmospheric mist threading between ancient pines, single beam of last light breaking through the canopy, deep shadows and rich violet-teal colour grading, Roger Deakins cinematography influence, anamorphic lens characteristic flare, film grain, Arrival (2016) colour palette, movie still --ar 2.39:1 --v 6.1 --stylize 250 --no modern elements, buildings, people

[Stylize 250: Balances your description with Midjourney's cinematic aesthetic. More dramatic than Stylize 150.]

VARIATION 3 — EPIC CONCEPT ART:
/imagine prompt: epic fantasy forest realm at twilight, impossibly tall ancient trees with glowing moss, ethereal blue mist swirling between gnarled roots, shafts of supernatural light piercing the canopy, mythic scale, artstation trending, Craig Mullins concept art style, digital painting, detailed and painterly, sense of ancient magic and mystery --ar 16:9 --v 6.1 --stylize 600 --no modern, realistic, photographic, urban

[Stylize 600: Maximum Midjourney aesthetic expression. Highly artistic, departs significantly from literal description.]

LIGHTING TIPS FOR BLUE HOUR:
- Blue hour lasts 20–30 minutes — specify 'just after sunset' or 'civil twilight' to get the right light quality
- Add 'long exposure photography style' for that characteristic smooth mist and water effect
- 'Atmospheric perspective' tells Midjourney to add depth haze, which dramatically improves landscape realism

💡 Pro Tips
Blue hour and golden hour are the most photogenic lighting conditions — specify the exact moment you want for consistent results
Add a camera and lens reference for photorealistic landscapes — 'shot on Sony A7R IV with 24mm lens' significantly improves detail quality
Atmospheric perspective (distance haze) is the single element that most improves landscape depth — always mention it for wide landscapes
The --no prompt is crucial for landscapes — common unwanted elements are 'people, vehicles, urban elements, text, watermark'
⚠️ Common Mistakes
Not specifying the time of day — lighting is 80% of landscape photography; vague lighting produces generic results
Too many style references in one prompt — Midjourney gets confused by conflicting aesthetic signals; pick one dominant style
Forgetting the aspect ratio — landscape photos look best at 16:9 or wider; square crops lose the panoramic feeling
High stylize values for photorealism — Stylize 150–200 keeps you close to the description; Stylize 500+ creates artistic interpretation that may not match your intent
❓ FAQ
  • What's the best --stylize value for photorealistic landscapes?
    100–200 for photorealism. This range follows your description closely while Midjourney still applies its natural photography training. Above 300, it starts adding 'Midjourney aesthetic' which is beautiful but less literally photographic.
  • How do I get water effects (misty lakes, smooth waterfalls)?
    Add 'long exposure photography' to your prompt. This triggers the training data associated with long-exposure landscape photography, producing the characteristic smooth water and cloud blur effects. Combine with 'ND filter photography' for extreme effect.
  • Can I use photographer names as style references?
    Yes — Ansel Adams (black and white landscape), Frans Lanting (wildlife/nature), Peter Lik (dramatic landscapes), Marc Adamus (adventure landscapes) are all understood by Midjourney. Be specific about which photographer and era.
  • How do I create a consistent landscape series?
    Use the same base prompt structure and only change one variable at a time (time of day, weather, season). Lock your --stylize value, --v version, and aspect ratio. This creates a coherent visual language across your series.
