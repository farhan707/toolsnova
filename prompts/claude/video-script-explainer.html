📋 Prompt
You are a video production writer who specialises in explainer videos that convert viewers into trial users.

Product/Concept: [product or concept]
Audience: [target audience]
Value prop: [core value proposition]
Length: [video length] seconds

Task: Write a complete explainer video script:

STRUCTURE (for a 60-second video — scale proportionally):
[0–5s] HOOK: A relatable problem moment — show, don't tell
[5–15s] PROBLEM AGITATION: Expand the problem briefly
[15–30s] SOLUTION INTRODUCTION: Introduce the product as the hero
[30–50s] HOW IT WORKS: 3 steps maximum, super simple
[50–60s] SOCIAL PROOF + CTA: One stat + one action

FORMAT:
- NARRATION: What the voiceover says
- [VISUAL]: What appears on screen
- [MUSIC NOTE]: Emotional direction
- Timing markers throughout

Constraints: 150 words per 60 seconds. No jargon. A 12-year-old should understand what the product does after watching.

💡 Pro Tips
The problem should be shown in action, not stated — 'you're staring at a blank screen' works better than 'AI prompts are hard'
Three steps maximum for 'how it works' — more steps suggest the product is complicated
The CTA in an explainer should match what you most want viewers to do — website visit, trial signup, or download
Test your script by reading it out loud while timing it — 150 words takes most people exactly 60 seconds to read
⚠️ Common Mistakes
Starting with the company or product name instead of the problem — you lose viewers in the first 3 seconds
Trying to explain every feature — pick one workflow and show it perfectly
Ending with 'Learn more' as the only CTA — it's the weakest CTA in marketing; be specific
Overloading the visual direction — simple animations with clear product UI beats complex motion graphics for clarity
❓ FAQ
  • How long should an explainer video be?
    60–90 seconds for SaaS/apps. 2–3 minutes for complex B2B products. Research shows 60-second videos retain 77% of viewers; 90-second videos drop to 57%. For homepage placement, 60 seconds or shorter is almost always better.
  • Should I use animation or live action?
    Animation is more flexible (can show the product from any angle), ages better, and is often cheaper for startups. Live action builds more personal trust. For software products, screen recording + animation hybrid tends to be the highest-converting format.
  • What voiceover style works best?
    Conversational and warm rather than 'professional announcer' voice. British accents test well for tech audiences globally. Match your brand voice — a fun product with a serious, formal voiceover creates cognitive dissonance.
  • Can this script work for different video lengths?
    Yes — scale proportionally. For 90 seconds, add more 'how it works' detail. For 30 seconds, cut everything except hook, solution name, and CTA. Specify your target length clearly in the prompt for best results.
