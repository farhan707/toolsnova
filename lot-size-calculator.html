About lot size calculation

Risk management
Never risk more than 1–2% per trade. This calculator ensures your position size matches your risk tolerance.
Pip value
For standard EUR/USD lots, pip value is ~$10. For gold (XAUUSD), use $1 per 0.01 move = $100/lot for $1 move.
Formula
Lot size = Risk Amount / (Stop Loss in pips × Pip Value per lot)

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a lot in forex?
1 standard lot = 100,000 units of base currency. 1 mini lot = 10,000 units. 1 micro lot = 1,000 units. Most retail brokers allow trading in 0.01 lot increments.
Why is position sizing critical?
Position sizing determines how much you lose on a bad trade. Risking 1% per trade means even 50 consecutive losses only reduces the account by ~40%. Risking 5% makes 20 losses catastrophic.
What is the 1% risk rule?
Risk no more than 1–2% of account balance per trade. On a $10,000 account, 1% = $100 maximum loss. This ensures a long losing streak doesn't wipe the account.
