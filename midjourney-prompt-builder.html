How to Write Perfect Midjourney Prompts

📐 Prompt Structure
The best Midjourney prompts follow: [Subject] + [Environment] + [Style] + [Lighting] + [Camera] + [Mood] + [Quality tags] + [Parameters]. More specific subject descriptions produce more consistent results than vague ones.
⚖️ Stylize Parameter
--stylize controls how strongly Midjourney applies its aesthetic. Low (0–100): stays close to your prompt. Medium (250–500): balanced interpretation. High (750–1000): highly stylized, more artistic freedom. Default is 100.
🎲 Chaos Parameter
--chaos (0–100) controls variation between results. Low chaos: consistent, predictable outputs. High chaos: wild, unexpected interpretations. Use low chaos for product/professional work, high chaos for creative exploration.
🚫 Negative Prompts
Use --no to exclude unwanted elements: --no blurry, text, watermark, ugly, deformed. In Midjourney v6, negative prompts are more effective than in earlier versions. Always exclude "text" unless you want text in the image.
🖌 Artist References
Adding "in the style of [artist]" or "by [artist]" is the fastest way to get a consistent aesthetic. Greg Rutkowski for epic fantasy, Artgerm for portraits, Simon Stalenhag for retrofuturism. Combine 2–3 artists for unique blends.
📸 Camera Terms
"Shot on 35mm Kodak Portra 400" gives film grain and color. "85mm f/1.4 bokeh" gives portrait blur. "Shot on Sony A7R V" gives sharp digital. Camera and lens specifications dramatically change the feel of photorealistic images.
💡 v6.1 tip: Midjourney v6.1 understands natural language much better than earlier versions. You can write full sentences for the subject description instead of comma-separated keywords. "A woman standing in the rain looking up at the sky" works better than "woman, rain, sky, looking up."

DALL-E 3 vs Midjourney vs Stable Diffusion

Each model has different strengths. Midjourney v6 produces the most aesthetically refined images and excels at artistic styles. DALL-E 3 (via ChatGPT) follows complex instructions most accurately and handles text in images well. Stable Diffusion (SDXL/Flux) is open-source, runs locally, and is best for fine-tuned control with LoRA models. Adobe Firefly is commercially safe — all outputs are licensed for commercial use.

For the best results, use this builder's model selector to switch prompt format — DALL-E 3 needs more descriptive natural language, while Stable Diffusion benefits from keyword-heavy prompts with weights like (subject:1.4).
