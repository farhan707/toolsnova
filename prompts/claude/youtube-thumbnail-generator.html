📋 Prompt
You are a YouTube thumbnail designer who has A/B tested 1,000+ thumbnails and knows what drives clicks.

Topic: [video topic]
Emotion: [target emotion — shock/curiosity/desire/fear/joy]
Style: [channel style — educational/entertainment/documentary/tutorial/vlog]
Text: [title text to include — max 4–6 words]

Task: Create 3 thumbnail concepts + 1 AI image prompt for each:

For each concept:
LAYOUT: Composition description (rule of thirds, where elements go)
COLOUR: Dominant palette + contrast strategy
FACE: If a person — exact expression, position, eye contact
TEXT: Exact text, font weight, colour, placement
EMOTION: What emotion the viewer feels in 0.5 seconds
AI PROMPT: Ready to paste into Midjourney or DALL-E 3

THUMBNAIL PSYCHOLOGY NOTES:
- Why each design stops the scroll
- Mobile preview (thumbnails are often seen at 120px wide)
📄 Example output
TOPIC: 'I tested 50 AI tools so you don't have to'
EMOTION: Curiosity + relief | STYLE: Educational | TEXT: 'I tested them ALL'

CONCEPT 1 — THE REACTION FACE:
Layout: Face fills left 60%, text on right third
Face: Open mouth, wide eyes (genuine surprise/shock expression), looking slightly off-camera at text
Colour: Bright yellow background (#FFD700), black text, red accent on key word
Text: 'I tested them ALL' — bold white on dark band, 'ALL' in red oversized
Emotion: 'What did they find?!'
Mobile: Face + 'ALL' visible at thumbnail size

AI PROMPT (Midjourney):
/imagine prompt: youtube thumbnail photo of person with expression of genuine surprise and wide eyes, slight open mouth, looking toward text, bold personality, bright yellow solid background, studio lighting, direct camera engagement, high energy expression, professional youtube creator aesthetic, 16:9 ratio, clean composition --ar 16:9 --v 6.1 --stylize 200

CONCEPT 2 — THE COMPARISON GRID:
Layout: 3×3 grid of AI tool logos on left, single 'winner' highlighted with glow on right
Colour: Dark background (#1a1a2e), vibrant tool colours, one glowing highlight
Text: 'The winner is...' pointing to highlighted tool
Emotion: 'I need to know which one'

THUMBNAIL PSYCHOLOGY:
Concept 1 wins on mobile (face + short text readable at 120px)
Concept 2 works for desktop-heavy audiences who recognise tool logos
Rule: If you can't tell what the video is about from the thumbnail at thumbnail size, redesign it

💡 Pro Tips
The face rule: thumbnails with human faces and strong emotion outperform non-face thumbnails by 40% on average
Use 3 colours maximum — more creates visual chaos at small sizes
Test your thumbnail at 120px × 68px before finalising — this is how it appears in mobile recommended feeds
High contrast between background and text is non-negotiable — light text on light background gets zero clicks
⚠️ Common Mistakes
Too much text — 4–6 words maximum; more is illegible at thumbnail size
Using the video's title as thumbnail text word-for-word — thumbnail and title should complement, not duplicate
Consistent thumbnail design that doesn't evolve — A/B test regularly
Generic stock-photo expressions — genuine, extreme, specific emotions outperform generic smiles
❓ FAQ
  • Should I always use a face in thumbnails?
    Human faces with emotion outperform non-face thumbnails for most content types. Exception: tutorial channels, screen recording content, or listicle videos where the content itself is the visual hook. Test both for your channel.
  • What's the ideal thumbnail size?
    YouTube recommends 1280×720 (16:9 HD). File size under 2MB. Formats: JPG, PNG, GIF, BMP, PNG. Always design at full resolution; the platform compresses it. Ensure readability at 120px wide.
  • How do I A/B test thumbnails?
    YouTube Studio's 'Test & Compare' feature (available to channels 1,000+ subscribers) lets you test 2 thumbnail versions and automatically selects the winner based on click-through rate. Run for at least 2 weeks to get statistically meaningful data.
  • Can AI generate production-ready thumbnails?
    AI can generate strong starting concepts (especially for background, atmosphere, and composition), but most YouTube creators still do final text placement and colour grading in Canva, Photoshop, or Figma for precise control. Use AI for the base image; finalise in a design tool.
