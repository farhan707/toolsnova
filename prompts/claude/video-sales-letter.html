Write a complete VSL script with timestamps:

[0:00-1:00] HOOK: Pattern interrupt + bold specific claim + who this is for
[1:00-4:00] STORY: Your journey from the same problem to the solution + credentials
[4:00-8:00] PROBLEM AGITATION: Name it specifically, consequences of not solving, destroy the main false belief
[8:00-13:00] SOLUTION: Reveal product, explain mechanism (WHY it works), features as benefits
[13:00-16:00] PROOF: Social proof + logical proof
[16:00-18:00] OFFER: What's included, value stack, price, guarantee
[18:00-20:00] CLOSE: Urgency if real, specific CTA repeated, cost of inaction reframe

💡 Pro Tips
The hook is everything — if viewers leave in the first 60 seconds, nothing else matters
Specificity sells — '2 hours per week' beats 'save time'; '50,000 users' beats 'used by thousands'
The guarantee addresses the specific fear behind the main objection — state it explicitly
Never manufacture scarcity — if you say 'only 50 spots', there must actually be 50 spots
⚠️ Common Mistakes
Starting with the product instead of the pain — viewers don't care until you've earned their attention
Features not benefits — nobody cares about 200 prompts; they care about saving 2 hours per week
Weak guarantee language — 'we might refund you' kills conversions
No mechanism — explaining WHY your solution works doubles believability
❓ FAQ
  • How long?
    Low-ticket (under £50): 10-15 min. Mid-ticket (£50-£500): 15-30 min. High-ticket (£500+): up to 60 min.
  • Camera or slides?
    On-camera typically converts better. A polished slides-with-voiceover VSL beats a poor-quality on-camera presentation. Start with what you can execute well.
  • Most important section?
    The hook (first 60-90 seconds). Then problem agitation — if viewers don't feel understood, they don't believe the solution.
  • ChatGPT or Claude?
    ChatGPT is slightly stronger — its directness and conversational energy match the format. Claude is better for high-ticket VSLs needing nuanced trust-building.
