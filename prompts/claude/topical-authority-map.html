📋 Prompt
You are an SEO strategist specialising in topical authority who has helped sites go from zero to 100K+ organic monthly visitors.

Niche: [website niche]
Main topic: [main topic to dominate]
Current content: [current content count] articles/pages
Domain age: [domain age]

Task: Build a complete topical authority map:

1. TOPICAL LANDSCAPE:
- Core topic (what you want to own)
- 5 subtopics (second-tier coverage areas)
- 3–5 related topics (adjacencies that build authority)

2. CONTENT ARCHITECTURE:
Pillar pages (1 per subtopic): Role and structure
Cluster pages (5–10 per pillar): Specific keywords to target
Supporting content (FAQs, glossaries, tools): What to build

3. AUTHORITY GAPS: Topics competitors cover that you don't yet

4. PUBLISHING ROADMAP: 6-month content calendar (one cluster per month)

5. INTERNAL LINKING PLAN: Which pages link to which

6. QUICK WINS (first 30 days): 5 pieces of content to publish immediately that have the best chance of ranking fast

💡 Pro Tips
Publish cluster pages before pillar pages — clusters provide the evidence for the pillar's authority claims
Topical authority is proven by covering the topic exhaustively at every level — if there's a question someone can ask about your topic, you should have an answer
Internal linking is 80% of what makes topical authority work in practice — every page should link to its pillar, and the pillar should link to every cluster
Update your authority map every 6 months — new keywords emerge, competitors move, and your gaps change
⚠️ Common Mistakes
Trying to build authority on too many topics simultaneously — pick one core topic and dominate it before expanding
Publishing pillar pages without cluster support — Google can't verify your authority claim without the surrounding evidence
Ignoring existing content — auditing what you already have is step 1; building on existing strength is faster than starting from scratch
Not tracking topical authority metrics — measure your keyword rankings within each subtopic cluster to see which pillars are building fastest
❓ FAQ
  • How long does it take to build topical authority?
    For most niches, 6–12 months of consistent publishing within a well-structured topical map. Sites with existing domain age and backlinks can see results faster. The compound effect means months 9–12 grow faster than months 1–3.
  • How many pillar pages do I need?
    Start with 2–3 pillars maximum. Each pillar needs 8–12 cluster pages to be credible. It's better to fully build out 2 pillars than to have 10 orphaned pillar pages without cluster support.
  • Is topical authority more important than backlinks?
    In 2026, many SEOs believe topical authority has become as important as, or more important than, raw backlink quantity for niche sites. A site with deep topical coverage often outranks a site with more backlinks but shallower coverage.
  • Can AI help with topical authority research?
    Yes — AI is excellent at generating keyword clusters and identifying subtopics you might have missed. For accurate search volume and competition data, validate the AI's suggestions in a real keyword tool like Ahrefs or Semrush.
