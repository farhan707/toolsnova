How to Use the Pip Value Calculator

  1. Select your currency pair — the pip value differs significantly between pairs depending on the quote currency.
  2. Enter lot size — the position size in standard lots (1.0 = standard, 0.1 = mini, 0.01 = micro).
  3. Enter account currency — if your account is in a currency other than USD, the pip value is converted automatically.
  4. Read pip value — the dollar (or account currency) value of a single pip move at your specified lot size.
  5. Use in position sizing — divide your risk amount by (stop loss pips × pip value per lot) to calculate your lot size.
📐 Quick reference: For USD-quote pairs (EURUSD, GBPUSD, AUDUSD): 1 pip = $10 per standard lot. For JPY pairs: 1 pip = ~$9.10 per standard lot. For Gold (XAUUSD): 1 pip ($0.01 price move) = $0.10 per standard lot, or 1 full dollar move = $10 per standard lot.

Understanding Pip Values

📏 What Is a Pip?
Pip stands for 'Percentage in Point' — the smallest standard price movement in forex. For most pairs: 0.0001 (4th decimal). For JPY pairs: 0.01 (2nd decimal). For Gold: $0.01 per troy ounce.
💱 USD Quote Pairs
EURUSD, GBPUSD, AUDUSD, NZDUSD — quote currency is USD. 1 pip = exactly $10 per standard lot regardless of current exchange rate. Most consistent for risk calculations.
💱 USD Base Pairs
USDJPY, USDCHF, USDCAD — USD is the base currency. Pip value in USD varies with the current exchange rate. At USDJPY 150.00: 1 pip = $10 ÷ 1.50 = $6.67 per standard lot.
💱 Cross Pairs
EURGBP, EURJPY, GBPJPY — neither currency is USD. Pip value must be calculated via the USD conversion rate of the quote currency. Most brokers handle this automatically.
📊 Pipette
Many brokers quote prices to 5 decimal places (pipettes). EURUSD at 1.08523 — the '3' is a pipette (1/10th of a pip). Always confirm whether your broker uses 4 or 5 decimal places when counting pip distances.
⚡ Importance for Risk
Pip value is the crucial link between stop loss distance (in pips) and dollar risk. At $10/pip/lot: 20-pip stop with 1 lot = $200 risk. 50-pip stop with 0.5 lots = $250 risk. Understanding pip value prevents position sizing errors.

Pip Value in Risk Management

Calculating exact dollar risk

Dollar Risk = Lot Size × Pip Value Per Lot × Stop Loss in Pips. Example: 0.5 lots EURUSD, $10 pip value, 30-pip stop = 0.5 × $10 × 30 = $150 risk. This calculation should be done mentally before every trade to confirm your risk amount matches your plan.

Pip value changes with account currency

If your trading account is denominated in GBP, EUR, or another currency, your pip values in account currency change with exchange rates. A prop firm account in EUR trading EURUSD: the pip value in EUR changes daily with the EURUSD rate. Most platforms show pip value in account currency automatically — verify your broker does this correctly.

Pip value for indices and commodities

Indices and commodities use 'points' rather than pips. NAS100: 1 point = $1 per mini lot. US30 (Dow): 1 point = $1 per mini lot. SPX500: 1 point = $10 per standard lot. Crude oil: 1 point = $10 per standard lot. Always verify point values with your specific broker as they can vary.

📊 Risk verification habit: Before clicking 'Buy' or 'Sell', say your risk out loud: 'This trade risks $X at Y pips stop on Z lots.' If this number is higher than your planned risk amount, reduce lot size before entering. This 5-second check prevents most accidental over-risk entries.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a pip?
A pip (percentage in point) is the smallest standard price move in a currency pair. For most pairs (EUR/USD, GBP/USD), 1 pip = 0.0001. For JPY pairs (USD/JPY, EUR/JPY), 1 pip = 0.01. For gold (XAU/USD), 1 pip = 0.01.
How is pip value calculated?
Pip Value = (Pip Size × Lot Size × Contract Size) × (Quote Currency / Account Currency). For EUR/USD with 1 standard lot: 0.0001 × 1 × 100,000 = $10 per pip.
Why does pip value matter?
Pip value tells you exactly how much money you gain or lose per pip movement. Without knowing pip value, you cannot accurately calculate position size, set stop losses in dollar terms, or manage risk properly.
Is pip value different for gold?
Yes. Gold (XAU/USD) has a contract size of 100 oz. A $1 move = 100 pips at $0.01 per pip. So 1 standard lot of gold: 1 pip = $0.01 × 100,000 = Wait — gold pip value = $0.01 × 100 oz = $1 per 0.01 move = $100 per $1 move per lot.
What is a pipette?
A pipette (fractional pip) is 1/10th of a pip. Most modern brokers quote 5 decimal places for regular pairs and 3 for JPY pairs. The 5th decimal place is the pipette. 10 pipettes = 1 pip.
