Understanding Height Measurement Systems

📏 Metric System (cm/m)
The international standard. 1 metre = 100 centimetres. Average adult height: men ~171cm globally, women ~159cm globally. Used in all medical and scientific contexts worldwide except the US. Simple decimal system makes calculations straightforward.
📏 Imperial System (ft/in)
Primarily used in the United States. 1 foot = 12 inches. 1 yard = 3 feet. Average US adult height: men ~5'9" (175cm), women ~5'4" (163cm). The foot-and-inch system requires conversion to decimal feet or total inches for most calculations.
🔢 Key Conversion Facts
1 inch = 2.54 centimetres exactly. 1 foot = 30.48 cm. 1 metre = 3.28084 feet = 39.3701 inches. Common heights: 5'0" = 152.4cm. 5'6" = 167.6cm. 5'9" = 175.3cm. 6'0" = 182.9cm. 6'3" = 190.5cm.
🏥 Medical Context
Medical records use metric in most countries. Drug dosing, BMI calculation, and growth chart percentiles all require metric measurements. When visiting doctors abroad or using international health apps, knowing your height in both systems prevents confusion and potential errors.
👗 Clothing and Shopping
Online clothing retailers from different countries use different size systems. European sizes use cm for height in trouser length and sleeve length. US clothing uses inches for inseam. Converting between systems ensures correct fit when buying internationally.
🏀 Sports Context
Basketball player heights are reported in feet/inches in the NBA, cm in European leagues. The same player is 6'7" in US media and 200cm in European reports. Understanding both systems is necessary for following international sports.

Height in Health and Context

Height and health

Height itself is not a modifiable health factor for adults — it is genetically determined and completed by early adulthood. However, height measurements are important for calculating BMI, medication dosing, assessing growth in children, and tracking growth disorders. Accurate height measurement requires: standing straight against a flat wall, heels together touching the wall, looking straight ahead, measuring at the top of the head without shoes.

Children's height tracking

In children, height tracking on growth charts is an important health monitoring tool. Pediatricians plot height and weight at each visit to track growth percentiles. A child consistently at the 50th percentile for height is average. Significant drops in percentile rank over time may indicate growth disorders, nutritional deficiencies, or other health conditions warranting evaluation.

Average heights globally

Global variation in average height is significant. Tallest countries: Netherlands (men avg 182.9cm), Montenegro, Denmark, Norway. Shortest average heights: Indonesia, Bolivia, Philippines (men avg ~160cm). These differences reflect genetic factors, nutrition, and healthcare access across generations. Average heights have increased dramatically in most countries over the past century due to improved nutrition.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I convert cm to feet and inches?
Divide centimetres by 2.54 to get total inches. Then divide by 12 to get feet, and the remainder is inches. Example: 175cm ÷ 2.54 = 68.9in = 5ft 8.9in ≈ 5'9".
How tall is 6 feet in cm?
6 feet = 72 inches = 182.88 cm ≈ 183 cm. Common conversions: 5'0" = 152cm, 5'6" = 168cm, 5'9" = 175cm, 6'0" = 183cm, 6'3" = 190cm.
What is average height worldwide?
Global average male height: ~171cm (5'7"). Global average female height: ~159cm (5'3"). Tallest countries: Netherlands, Montenegro, Bosnia (men avg ~183cm). Shortest: Indonesia, Bolivia, Philippines (men avg ~160cm).
