How to Use the Typing Speed Test

  1. Click Start — the test begins when you start typing. A passage of text is displayed for you to type.
  2. Type the text accurately — errors are highlighted in real time. Backspace to correct mistakes.
  3. Complete the passage — the timer stops when you finish typing the full text.
  4. Read your results — see WPM (words per minute), accuracy percentage, and CPM (characters per minute).
  5. Practise regularly — take the test daily for 2–4 weeks to see significant improvement. Most people can improve 20–30 WPM with consistent practice.
⌨️ WPM calculation: WPM = (total characters typed ÷ 5) ÷ minutes. Dividing by 5 standardises 'words' to 5 characters each — regardless of actual word length. This standardisation allows fair comparison across different texts and languages. A 'word' in WPM = exactly 5 keystrokes, not an actual dictionary word.

Understanding Typing Speed

📊 WPM Benchmarks
Beginner: 20–40 WPM. Average: 40–60 WPM. Above average: 60–80 WPM. Professional: 80–100 WPM. Fast: 100–120 WPM. Expert: 120+ WPM. World record (stenography): 360 WPM. Average office worker: 40 WPM. Professional typists: 75–100 WPM. Average programmer: 50–60 WPM (slower due to special characters).
🎯 Accuracy vs Speed
Accuracy is more important than raw speed. A typist at 80 WPM with 98% accuracy is more productive than 100 WPM with 90% accuracy — the slower typist produces fewer errors requiring correction. Professional touch typists typically maintain 97–99% accuracy. Focus on accuracy first; speed naturally increases with practice as accurate patterns become habitual.
⌨️ Touch Typing
Touch typing: using all 10 fingers without looking at the keyboard, guided by muscle memory and home row position (ASDF for left hand, JKL; for right hand). Dramatically faster and less fatiguing than hunt-and-peck typing. Learning touch typing takes 2–4 weeks of practice but provides 50–100% speed improvement. Keybr.com and typing.com offer structured touch typing courses.
🔤 Keyboard Layouts
QWERTY: standard layout, not optimised for typing efficiency (designed to prevent typewriter jams). Dvorak: alternative layout with vowels on left home row, claims 30–40% reduction in finger movement. Colemak: modern alternative, more compatible with QWERTY shortcuts. Research on productivity difference is mixed — switching from QWERTY requires significant relearning time with uncertain benefit.
💻 Programming Typing
Programmers type differently from writers: frequent use of special characters ({}, [], (), ;), case switches, and keyboard shortcuts. Many programming-specific speed tests measure speed in 'code' rather than prose. Vim and Emacs keyboard bindings dramatically reduce required typing through powerful editing commands. IDE autocomplete and code snippets effectively multiply typing speed for common patterns.
📈 Typing Speed and Productivity
For knowledge workers, typing speed directly impacts output capacity. A writer at 60 WPM can produce approximately 3,600 words per hour of continuous typing. At 40 WPM: 2,400 words per hour. The 50% speed difference represents 50% more written output. For email-heavy roles, higher WPM reduces email response time and increases perceived responsiveness — a real career advantage.

Improving Typing Speed

The deliberate practice approach

Random typing practice produces slow improvement. Deliberate practice targeting specific weaknesses produces rapid improvement. Identify your slowest keys using a typing analytics tool (Keybr.com shows per-key speed). Practise those specific keys with targeted exercises. Most people's speed is limited by 5–10 specific keys or key combinations — improving these unlocks significant overall speed gains. 15–20 minutes of focused daily practice produces measurable improvement within 1–2 weeks.

Touch typing transition

If you currently hunt-and-peck or use fewer than 8 fingers, switching to full touch typing provides the largest long-term speed gain. The transition involves: 1) Learn home row position (ASDF JKL;). 2) Force yourself to use correct finger for each key — even if slower initially. 3) Use typing tutor software (Keybr, TypingClub, Typing.com) for structured lessons. 4) Expect 2–4 weeks at reduced speed before your new technique matches your old speed, then continuous improvement beyond your previous ceiling.

Keyboard hardware impact

Keyboard quality affects both speed and comfort. Mechanical keyboards (Cherry MX switches, similar) provide tactile feedback that helps typists know when a key is registered, reducing errors and often increasing speed. Key travel distance, actuation force, and noise level are personal preferences — most fast typists prefer medium-weight keys with clear tactile feedback. Ergonomic keyboards (split, tented) reduce injury risk for heavy typists without necessarily increasing speed. Membrane keyboards (most laptop and budget keyboards) are generally adequate for average speeds.

⌨️ Typing speed improvement plan: Week 1: take baseline test, learn/reinforce touch typing technique. Week 2: 15 min daily practice on Keybr.com targeting slow keys. Week 3: full text typing practice, focus on accuracy over speed. Week 4: retest and compare. Most people see 15–25 WPM improvement in 4 weeks of consistent daily practice. Maintenance: 10 min practice 3× per week to continue improving.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is WPM?
WPM (Words Per Minute) is the standard measure of typing speed. It's calculated as (characters typed ÷ 5) ÷ minutes elapsed. Dividing by 5 accounts for the average word length in English. A score of 40 WPM is average, 60 WPM is good, and 80+ WPM is excellent.
What is a good typing speed?
Average adult typing speed is 40 WPM. Office workers typically type 50–70 WPM. Professional typists reach 75–90 WPM. The world record is over 200 WPM. For most jobs, 60+ WPM is sufficient. Programmers average 60–80 WPM but often type in bursts.
How do I improve my typing speed?
Touch typing — using all 10 fingers without looking at the keyboard — is the most effective method. Start slow and focus on accuracy; speed follows naturally. Practice daily for 10–15 minutes. Home row positioning (ASDF JKL;) is the foundation of fast, accurate typing.
Why does my accuracy matter more than speed?
Accuracy reduces errors that need correction. At 95% accuracy, you're only retyping 1 in 20 characters. At 80% accuracy, you're correcting 1 in 5. Consistent 95%+ accuracy at moderate speed is more productive than high speed with many errors.
Is my typing data sent anywhere?
No. The typing test runs entirely in your browser. No keystrokes, text, or results are stored or transmitted anywhere.
