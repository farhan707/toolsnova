📋 Prompt
You are a strategic business consultant specialising in competitive analysis and strategic planning.

Company/Project: [company or project name]
Industry: [industry]
Main competitors: [main competitors]
Time horizon: [time horizon — next 12 months / 3 years / 5 years]

Task: Create a comprehensive SWOT analysis including:

STRENGTHS (internal, positive) — 5–7 genuine competitive advantages
WEAKNESSES (internal, negative) — 4–6 honest limitations (don't sugarcoat)
OPPORTUNITIES (external, positive) — 5 market trends or gaps to exploit
THREATS (external, negative) — 4–5 real risks (competitors/regulation/market shifts)

STRATEGIC RECOMMENDATIONS:
- SO Strategy (use strengths + opportunities): 2 actions
- WO Strategy (use opportunities to fix weaknesses): 2 actions
- ST Strategy (use strengths to counter threats): 2 actions
- WT Strategy (minimise weaknesses, avoid threats): 2 actions

PRIORITISED ACTION PLAN: Top 3 strategic priorities for [time horizon]

Format: Clear SWOT table, then strategic recommendations, then action plan.

💡 Pro Tips
Make weaknesses honest — a SWOT where every weakness is minor and every threat is distant is useless for strategic planning
Opportunities should be external trends, not things you plan to do — 'increase marketing budget' is a strategy, not an opportunity
Weight the quadrants by what's most time-sensitive for your situation
Revisit your SWOT every 6 months — both the analysis and the strategies should evolve as you learn
⚠️ Common Mistakes
Treating SWOT as a one-time exercise rather than a living strategic tool
Listing aspirations as strengths ('innovative culture') without evidence
Confusing weaknesses (internal) with threats (external)
Creating a SWOT without acting on the strategic recommendations
❓ FAQ
  • Should I do a SWOT for every project?
    For significant decisions (new product, new market, major investment), yes. For routine operations, a quarterly strategic review is usually sufficient. The effort should match the stakes.
  • Who should be involved in creating a SWOT?
    The most useful SWOTs combine insider knowledge (team) with outside perspective (advisors, customers). Pure internal SWOTs often miss blind spots in the weaknesses and threats quadrants.
  • What's the difference between SWOT and PESTLE?
    SWOT covers internal (Strengths/Weaknesses) and external (Opportunities/Threats) factors broadly. PESTLE (Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Legal, Environmental) focuses exclusively on macro-external factors and is often used as input to the SWOT.
  • Can I use Gemini for SWOT analysis?
    Yes. Gemini is particularly good at identifying market trends and opportunities for the O and T quadrants. For honest internal weaknesses, you'll need to provide more specific context about your business.
