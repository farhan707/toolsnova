📋 Prompt
You are a comic book creator and character designer who has developed characters for indie and major publishers.

Character concept: [hero/villain/sidekick/morally complex]
Genre: [superhero/noir/sci-fi/fantasy/horror/slice-of-life]
Art style: [Marvel realist/manga/indie/Moebius/Kirby]
Purpose: [comic book/game/illustration/writing project]

Create a complete character design package:

1. OVERVIEW: Name, origin, one-sentence essence
2. VISUAL DESCRIPTION: Body type, height, facial features, costume with hex colour codes, materials, details, and the SIGNATURE ELEMENT — what makes them unmistakable in black silhouette
3. BACKSTORY (300 words): Origin, formative event, what drives them
4. PERSONALITY AND VOICE: 3 core traits + 1 fatal flaw; how they speak (3 example lines); what they want vs. what they need
5. POWERS/SKILLS: Primary ability, secondary skills, limitation/weakness
6. VISUAL REFERENCES: 3 existing characters they draw from (and exactly how they differ)
7. AI IMAGE PROMPTS (3): Character portrait, action pose, civilian/casual version

💡 Pro Tips
Silhouette test is the most important design check — if you can't identify your character in black silhouette alone, the design needs a stronger signature element
The fatal flaw must be connected to the power or backstory — a general bad temper is generic; a flaw that grows from the origin is design
Write dialogue before description — voice reveals character more than any visual detail
Hex codes for costume colours are worth specifying for AI prompts — colour is the fastest way to build recognition across scenes
⚠️ Common Mistakes
Too powerful with no meaningful limitation — powers without costs aren't dramatically interesting
Generic backstory ('parents died, wants revenge') — the formative event should create the specific psychological wound
Over-designed costume — the most iconic characters have simple strong silhouettes, not 47 details
Inconsistent power logic — define the rules and stick to them; inconsistency breaks reader trust
❓ FAQ
  • How do I make a character feel original?
    Combine two unexpected things — competence and emotional fragility; a villain right about the problem but wrong about the solution. Start with a specific human contradiction, then build the concept around it.
  • What makes a good fatal flaw?
    A strength taken too far, or a wound that drives bad decisions. Pride becomes arrogance. Loyalty becomes blind obedience. The origin story wound should directly generate the flaw.
  • Best model?
    Claude builds psychologically consistent characters, writes distinctive dialogue, and maintains thematic coherence between backstory, power, and flaw.
  • Best AI image tool for character sheets?
    Midjourney for cinematic portraits. DALL-E 3 for consistent appearance across multiple poses. Use Midjourney's Character Reference (--cref) for consistency across a series.
