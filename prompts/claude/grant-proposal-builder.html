📋 Prompt
You are a professional grant writer who has secured over 5 million in funding for nonprofits and social enterprises.

Organisation: [name and type]
Funder: [government/foundation/corporate/EU/other]
Amount: [amount you are applying for]
Project: [name and 2-sentence description]
Beneficiaries: [who benefits and how many]
Problem: [the specific problem this project addresses]
Track record: [relevant previous work or achievements]

Task:
1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (300 words): Problem, solution, impact, and funding ask
2. STATEMENT OF NEED (500 words): Evidence-backed problem with data, local context, and beneficiary voice
3. PROJECT NARRATIVE (600 words): What you will do, how, who delivers it, and theory of change
4. SMART OBJECTIVES: 3-5 objectives with measurable indicators
5. BUDGET NARRATIVE: Key line item justifications and demonstration of cost-effectiveness
6. EVALUATION PLAN: How you will measure and report impact
7. ORGANISATIONAL CREDIBILITY: Why you are the right organisation to deliver this
8. RED FLAGS TO AVOID: What would cause this application to be rejected
📄 Example output
GRANT PROPOSAL: Code Ready Programme — 45000 NLCF

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY:
In Oldham, 18.2% of young people aged 16-24 are NEET — nearly double the national average. The primary barrier: 62% of local job vacancies require digital skills that NEET young people have never been taught.

Bright Futures Youth CIC requests 45000 to deliver Code Ready — a free intensive 12-week coding and digital skills programme for 60 young people per year. Based on our track record with 180 previous participants with 68% into employment or education within 6 months, we expect at least 40 of the 60 participants to progress to employment, apprenticeship, or further education.

SMART OBJECTIVES:
1. Deliver Code Ready to 60 NEET young people by March 2027 — measured by attendance register
2. 75% of participants (45) achieve a nationally recognised digital qualification by programme end
3. 65% of participants (39) progress to employment, apprenticeship, or further education within 6 months

ORGANISATIONAL CREDIBILITY:
Bright Futures has delivered digital skills programming since 2021, reaching 180 young people across 3 cohorts. Our 68% positive destination rate compares favourably to the national average of 54% for comparable programmes. We employ 4 full-time DBS-cleared staff with ILM-qualified tutors.

💡 Pro Tips
Funder priorities come first — read the funding criteria before writing a single word, then use their language throughout
Evidence of need should be local and recent — local data about your specific community is more compelling than national statistics
Match your writing tone to the funder — NLCF should feel warm and community-focused; UKRI should feel rigorous and evidence-based
Brief the programme officer informally before submitting if possible — their guidance shapes a stronger application
⚠️ Common Mistakes
Describing your organisation before explaining the problem — funders fund problems not organisations
Vague impact statements — improved wellbeing is not an outcome; 68% into employment within 6 months is
Budget that does not explain the rationale — staffing costs need to show who, at what rate, for how many hours
Generic proposal that does not align with the funder's current strategic priorities — rejected immediately
❓ FAQ
  • How do I find grants?
    UK: NLCF Grants for Good Causes, government Find a Grant database, local community foundations, corporate CSR programmes. EU: Horizon Europe, Erasmus+. Subscription databases: Grants Online, Funding Central.
  • What is a theory of change?
    Maps Activities to Outputs to Outcomes to Impact. Funders want to see that your activities logically lead to your claimed outcomes.
  • How long should a grant proposal be?
    Follow funder word limits exactly — exceeding them can mean automatic disqualification. Executive summary: 1 page. Statement of need: 1-2 pages. Project narrative: 2-3 pages.
  • Best model?
    Claude is significantly stronger for grant writing — maintains formal evidence-based register, builds logical theory of change arguments, and uses appropriate hedging language for outcome projections.
