💡 Pro Tips
A/B test your title tags in Google Search Console — small changes can move CTR by 20–30%
Include a number when it's truthful — '7 Ways' outperforms 'Ways' in CTR studies
Write the meta description as if it's a Google ad — it's unpaid ad copy
Avoid ALL CAPS — it reads as shouting and reduces CTR in studies
⚠️ Common Mistakes
Duplicate title tags across multiple pages — Google may rewrite them or choose not to rank them
Going over 60 characters — the title gets truncated and looks broken in search results
Generic meta descriptions like 'Click here to learn more' — they convert poorly
Keyword stuffing the title: 'SEO SEO Consultant SEO Services London' — Google rewrites these
❓ FAQ
  • Does Google always use my meta description?
    No. Google rewrites meta descriptions approximately 60–70% of the time to better match the user's search query. This is normal. Write a good description anyway — it still improves CTR when Google does use it.
  • Does the title tag still matter for rankings in 2026?
    Yes, title tags remain a confirmed on-page ranking factor. They also directly affect your click-through rate in the SERP, which is a significant indirect ranking signal.
  • What's the difference between OG tags and meta tags?
    Meta tags (title + description) control how your page appears in Google. OG (Open Graph) tags control how your page appears when shared on social media. Both are important and can have different optimised content.
  • Should the title tag match the H1?
    They should be related but not identical. The title tag is optimised for search results; the H1 can be more descriptive or conversational once the user has already clicked.
