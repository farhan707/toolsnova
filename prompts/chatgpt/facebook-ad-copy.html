📋 Prompt
You are a direct-response copywriter who has managed $10M+ in Meta ad spend and knows what actually converts.

Product/Service: [product or service]
Target audience: [target audience]
Key benefit: [key benefit]
Offer: [offer — discount/free trial/free shipping/bonus]

Task: Write 5 Facebook/Instagram ad variations using different hooks:

FORMAT FOR EACH AD:
Primary text: (125 chars ideal — hook + value + CTA)
Headline: (40 chars — benefit-led)
Description: (30 chars — proof or urgency)
CTA button: [Shop Now/Learn More/Sign Up/Get Quote]

HOOK TYPES:
Ad 1 — Pain point: ('Tired of [problem]?')
Ad 2 — Social proof: ('[Number] people already...')
Ad 3 — Curiosity: ('The [thing] most [audience] never try...')
Ad 4 — Direct offer: ('[Offer] — ends [date]')
Ad 5 — Question: ('What if you could [outcome] without [objection]?')

Constraints: No exclamation marks in every line. Lead with emotion, close with logic. One CTA per ad.

💡 Pro Tips
Test one variable at a time — if you change the hook and the image simultaneously, you won't know what caused the difference
Video ads consistently outperform static in 2026 — this copy works as a video script overlay too
Your first line determines whether the 'See more' button gets clicked — most people never see beyond line 1
Narrow targeting outperforms broad targeting for most products — be specific about who this is for
⚠️ Common Mistakes
Using stock photos of people who look like models — authentic UGC-style images outperform by 3–5x in 2026
Running ads without a dedicated landing page — sending traffic to your homepage kills conversions
Never refreshing creative — ad fatigue sets in within 2–4 weeks for most audiences
Writing the same copy for Facebook and Instagram — the audiences behave differently
❓ FAQ
  • How many ads should I test at once?
    For most budgets, test 3–5 variations per ad set. Facebook's algorithm needs time to learn — too many variations splits the data and slows learning phase exit.
  • What's a good CTR for Facebook ads?
    A CTR above 1% is solid for most industries. Above 2% is excellent. Below 0.5% usually indicates a targeting or creative problem. CTR varies significantly by industry and placement.
  • Can I use this for Instagram Reels ads?
    Yes — but adjust the primary text for Reels. Reels ads overlay the first line of text on the video. Make your hook work as a standalone sentence without the rest of the copy.
  • Does Claude write better ad copy than ChatGPT?
    Both are capable. ChatGPT tends to write more punchy, direct copy. Claude follows nuanced constraints more precisely. Try both and pick the variations with the best hooks.
