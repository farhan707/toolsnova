📋 Prompt
You are an experienced YouTube scriptwriter who understands viewer retention psychology and the YouTube algorithm in 2026.

Video topic: [video topic]
Target audience: [target audience]
Video length: [video length] minutes
Channel style: [channel style — educational/entertaining/documentary/tutorial/reaction]

Task: Write a complete YouTube script including:
- HOOK (first 30 seconds): Pattern interrupt → Promise → Preview. Must make the viewer say 'I need to watch this whole thing'
- INTRO (30–60 seconds): Brief credentials + what they'll learn + subscribe ask
- MAIN CONTENT: Clear sections matching the video length
- MIDROLL HOOK at the 40% mark to re-engage viewers about to drop off
- OUTRO: Summary + CTA (like, subscribe, specific next video)

Format: [SECTION TITLE] headers. Action notes in [brackets]. Write for speaking, not reading.
Constraints: Assume the viewer's cursor is hovering over the back button at all times. Every sentence must earn the next.
📄 Example output
[HOOK — 0:00–0:30]

[Look directly at camera, slight pause before speaking]

I spent 6 months building the wrong type of business — and the weird thing is, I followed every piece of advice I could find. Today I'm going to show you exactly what I was missing, and why most online business advice is optimised for the person giving it, not for you.

[Text on screen: '6 months. $0 revenue.']

Stick around because in the next 12 minutes, you're going to see three specific mistakes that almost every first-time online business owner makes — including one that cost me $4,000 that I could have avoided in the first week.

[INTRO — 0:30–1:00]

Quick intro: I'm [name]. I now run [business] and help [audience]. But before I figured that out, I made the mistakes I'm about to show you — which is exactly why I can explain them so specifically...

💡 Pro Tips
Write the hook last — after you've written the whole script, you'll know what's actually most compelling
Read your script out loud while recording a test video — written language and spoken language are very different
The first 30 seconds determines 80% of your retention — spend 30% of your writing time on the hook
Use 'the reason I'm telling you this' as a transition — it's the most powerful re-engagement phrase in YouTube scripts
⚠️ Common Mistakes
Starting with 'Hey guys, welcome back to my channel' — drop the filler immediately
Spending too long on credentials — viewers don't care who you are until they care what you can do for them
Writing in paragraphs — speak in sentences, with pauses built in
Not scripting the outro — most creators lose 30–40% of potential subscribers by ending abruptly
❓ FAQ
  • Should I use a full script or bullet points?
    For beginners and complex topics, a full script with action notes works better. Experienced creators often use a detailed outline. The key is not sounding scripted — that comes from practice and delivery style.
  • How does this work with TikTok or Reels?
    For short-form video (under 90 seconds), use the TikTok Script Generator prompt instead — the structure is fundamentally different.
  • Can Gemini write YouTube scripts?
    Yes. Gemini 2.0 Flash is excellent for YouTube scripts. Try the instruction: 'write for a [channel name]-style delivery' if you want to match a specific creator's energy.
  • How long should my script be for a 10-minute video?
    An average speaking pace is 130–150 words per minute. A 10-minute video = roughly 1,400 words of script. Adjust for your natural speaking speed.
