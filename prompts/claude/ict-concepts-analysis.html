💡 Pro Tips
The ICT daily bias overrides everything — never take short setups on a bullish bias day regardless of lower TF structure
The Power of Three manipulation (London open) is your entry signal — learn to wait for it
Equal highs and equal lows are liquidity magnets — price is almost always engineered to sweep them before reversing
OTE entry without MSS confirmation is speculation; with MSS it becomes an ICT setup
⚠️ Common Mistakes
Taking setups against the daily bias — fighting the current
Entering at first touch of OB without waiting for MSS — price often sweeps through first
Trading outside kill zones — ICT targets London and NY specifically
Ignoring higher PD arrays — if a daily OB is above your short entry, price will visit it first
❓ FAQ
  • What is ICT methodology?
    Developed by Michael J. Huddleston. Focuses on how institutions accumulate and distribute positions. Key concepts: Order Blocks, Fair Value Gaps, Optimal Trade Entry, Power of Three, Kill Zones, and liquidity raids.
  • How does ICT differ from SMC?
    SMC is largely derived from ICT concepts. Purist ICT uses Huddleston's specific terminology and models. Both analyse market structure, liquidity, and institutional order flow — language and specific models vary slightly.
  • What timeframes does ICT recommend?
    Top-down: Monthly/Weekly for long-term bias → Daily for trade bias → H4 for structure → H1 for entry model → M15 for confirmation → M5 for precision entry.
  • Can AI analyse ICT concepts reliably?
    AI applies ICT frameworks well when given specific price data. Quality depends on specificity of your inputs — exact swing high/low levels and current price relative to key zones. AI cannot see live charts; you provide the numerical context.
