How FIFA Distributes World Cup 2026 Prize Money

💰 $871M Total Pool
FIFA revised the prize pool upward twice during planning — originally $655M in December 2025, then $727M, then finally $871M after the May 2026 FIFA Council Meeting in Vancouver. The increase came from better-than-expected broadcasting rights revenue. This is the largest prize pool in team sports history.
🏛️ Money Goes to Federations
Prize money is paid to national football associations (federations), not directly to players. Each federation then decides how to distribute it — typically with bonuses for players and coaching staff. Player bonus agreements vary widely: some federations pay 20–30% to the squad, others more. The split is negotiated before the tournament and varies by country.
🎯 Preparation Grant
All 48 teams receive a $2.5 million preparation grant before the tournament begins — regardless of results. This covers training camps, preparation matches, and travel. Added to the performance-based prize, every team earns a minimum of $11.5 million ($2.5M prep + $9M group stage) just for qualifying and competing.
🆕 Round of 32 Payout
The new Round of 32 — a stage that never existed at any previous World Cup — pays $11 million to teams eliminated there. This creates an extra $2 million incentive beyond the $9 million group stage payout, making qualification from the group stage financially significant even for smaller nations with limited football budgets.
🏆 Champion's $50M
The 2026 World Cup winner earns $50 million in prize money plus $2.5 million preparation fee = $52.5 million total. This is $8 million more than Argentina earned winning the 2022 World Cup. The runner-up earns $33 million. The difference between winning and losing the final is $17 million — the most financially significant moment in team sports.
📈 Historical Growth
World Cup prize money growth: 2010 South Africa $420M → 2014 Brazil $576M → 2018 Russia $400M (reduced) → 2022 Qatar $440M → 2026 USA/Mexico/Canada $871M. The 2026 pool represents a 98% increase from 2022. FIFA projects the 2030 World Cup (Morocco/Spain/Portugal) to exceed $1 billion for the first time.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does the World Cup 2026 winner earn?
The FIFA World Cup 2026 champion earns $50 million in prize money plus $2.5 million in preparation fees, for a total of $52.5 million. This is paid to the national football association. Player bonuses are negotiated separately by each federation — typically players receive 20–30% of the total prize money, though this varies significantly by country.
How much does a team get for qualifying to the World Cup 2026?
Every team that qualifies for the 2026 World Cup receives a $2.5 million preparation grant. If they are eliminated in the group stage, they earn an additional $9 million in prize money. Total minimum earnings: $11.5 million per team regardless of results in the tournament itself.
How much bigger is the 2026 World Cup prize pool vs 2022?
The 2026 World Cup prize pool of $871 million is almost exactly double the $440 million distributed at the 2022 Qatar World Cup. The increase reflects both the expanded 48-team format (more teams competing) and record-breaking broadcasting rights revenue generated by the expanded tournament.
Do players get the prize money directly?
No. FIFA pays prize money directly to the national football federation (association) of each country, not to individual players or coaches. The federation then distributes it according to pre-agreed bonus structures with the squad. These agreements vary significantly — some federations are more generous with players than others. The entire prize money goes to the federation's bank account first.
