📋 Prompt
You are a career transition coach who has helped 300+ professionals change careers without taking a pay cut.

Current role: [current role and industry]
Target role: [target role and industry]
Experience: [years in current field]
Timeline: [3/6/12 months]
Constraints: [can't quit yet/need salary/location/family]

Task:
1. TRANSFERABLE SKILLS: What directly transfers + what hiring managers in [target field] value immediately
2. SKILLS GAP: What's missing + fastest way to close each
3. TRANSITION NARRATIVE: Frame pivot as strength — 60-second answer to 'Why are you making this change?'
4. BRIDGE STRATEGY: Build credibility before you have the title — 3 things in the next 30 days
5. JOB SEARCH: Most transition-friendly companies/roles + how to get the first conversation
6. FINANCIAL PLAN: Realistic timeline to matching salary + roles that minimise the pay dip
7. 90-DAY QUICK START: Days 1–30, 31–60, 61–90 with specific actions
📄 Example output
TRANSITION: Marketing Manager → Product Manager

TRANSFERABLE SKILLS:
• Customer research and segmentation — PM teams need this; most PMs lack real depth here
• Quantitative analysis (attribution, campaign metrics) — maps directly to product metrics
• Stakeholder communication — briefing leadership, managing agencies = managing cross-functional teams
• Launch experience — coordinating marketing launches is half of what PMs do; name this explicitly

SKILLS GAP:
1. Technical fluency → CS50 (free, 12 weeks) or Product School fundamentals
2. No product ownership → PM a small internal project (website rebuild, tool migration)
3. No PRD writing → Write 3 PRDs for hypothetical product improvements; build a portfolio

TRANSITION NARRATIVE:
'I've spent 6 years understanding what customers want and communicating that internally and externally. I've been doing half the PM job without the title. I'm moving into roles where I can own the product decisions, not just the marketing. My customer research depth is something most PMs spend years trying to develop.'

BRIDGE (next 30 days):
1. Lead company's next website feature decision — write brief + research + recommendation as a product initiative
2. Join PM community (Lenny's Slack, Product Hunt) and contribute genuinely
3. Do 5 user interviews on a product you use; write findings as a product opportunity document

90-DAY PLAN:
Days 1–30: Skills audit + gap plan + start CS50 + LinkedIn repositioning
Days 31–60: Complete CS50 + 2 portfolio PRDs + 10 PM community connections
Days 61–90: First PM applications (associate/junior at startups) + 5 informational interviews

💡 Pro Tips
Target startups and small companies first — they value transferable skills more than large companies
Your transition narrative must frame the pivot as 'more of the same from a different angle'
Build in public — write about your transition on LinkedIn; builds network and signals commitment
Your first role doesn't have to be your dream role — optimise for getting your foot in the door
⚠️ Common Mistakes
Applying for senior roles in the new field before establishing credibility
Waiting until 'ready' — start bridge activities in your current role now
Weak narrative not proactively addressing 'why now?' and 'why this?'
Ignoring network — 70–80% of positions filled through network; informational interviews beat applications
❓ FAQ
  • Must I take a pay cut?
    Often in the short term — expect 10–30% below current salary. Most people return to or exceed previous salary within 2–3 years in the new field.
  • Need certifications?
    Depends on field. Tech/PM: certifications help. Finance: often required. Coding: portfolio beats certificates. Research what hiring managers actually look for.
  • Too late to change at 40?
    No. Transitions at 40+ are increasingly common. Accumulated skills, judgment, and network are real advantages. The narrative challenge is real but addressable.
  • Best model?
    Claude — realistic timelines, nuanced personalised advice, avoids generic career coaching platitudes.
