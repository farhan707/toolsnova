Understanding Data Size Units

💾 Bits and Bytes
Bit: the smallest unit of digital information — 0 or 1. Byte = 8 bits: the standard unit for data storage (one ASCII character = 1 byte). All storage sizes are multiples of bytes. Network speeds are often in bits per second (Mbps, Gbps) while file sizes are in bytes (MB, GB) — causing confusion: a 100 Mbps connection transfers 100/8 = 12.5 MB per second.
📊 Binary vs Decimal
Binary (IEC standard): 1 KiB = 1,024 bytes (2¹⁰). Used by OS file systems, RAM. Decimal (SI standard): 1 KB = 1,000 bytes (10³). Used by storage manufacturers, network speeds. For small files, difference is negligible. For large drives: 1 TB (decimal) = 0.909 TiB (binary) — explaining why a 1 TB drive shows as ~931 GB in Windows.
🔢 Size Scale
1 Byte = 8 bits. 1 KB ≈ 1 thousand bytes. 1 MB ≈ 1 million bytes. 1 GB ≈ 1 billion bytes. 1 TB ≈ 1 trillion bytes. 1 PB ≈ 1 quadrillion bytes. 1 EB ≈ 1 quintillion bytes. 1 ZB ≈ 1 sextillion bytes. Global internet traffic now measured in zettabytes per year. Total data generated by humanity: estimated at 120 zettabytes by 2025.
📱 File Size Reference
SMS message: ~0.1–0.2 KB. Email (text): ~20 KB. Web page: 1–5 MB average. MP3 song (128 kbps, 4 min): ~4 MB. HD photo (DSLR): 5–25 MB. 4K video (1 min): 350–400 MB. DVD: 4.7 GB. Blu-ray: 25–50 GB. Operating system: 5–20 GB. Modern video game: 50–200 GB.
🌐 Network Speed vs Storage
Network speeds: bits per second (bps, Kbps, Mbps, Gbps). File sizes: bytes (KB, MB, GB). Converting: download time (seconds) = file size (bytes) ÷ speed (bytes/second) = file size (MB) ÷ (speed Mbps ÷ 8). A 1 GB file on 100 Mbps connection: 1,000 MB ÷ (100 ÷ 8) = 1,000 ÷ 12.5 = 80 seconds. ISPs advertise in Mbps; you experience downloads in MB/s.
☁️ Cloud Storage Context
Free tiers: Google Drive 15 GB, iCloud 5 GB, Dropbox 2 GB, OneDrive 5 GB. Paid: typically $2–10/month per 100 GB–2 TB. Mobile data plans: 5–100 GB/month (cellular). The average smartphone user generates 3–5 GB of photos/year. A 4K movie is 15–25 GB. Understanding sizes helps evaluate whether storage plans meet actual needs.

Data Size in Technology

Understanding internet speeds

'100 Mbps broadband' means 100 megabits per second — not megabytes. Divide by 8 to get megabytes per second: 100 Mbps = 12.5 MB/s. Practical download speeds: a 1 GB game update at 100 Mbps takes approximately 80 seconds (assuming no overhead). Gigabit internet (1 Gbps) provides 125 MB/s — downloading that 1 GB game in 8 seconds. Upload speeds are typically 10–30% of download speeds on asymmetric connections like cable or DSL; fibre broadband often offers symmetrical speeds.

Storage planning for photography and video

Professional photographers and videographers need to plan storage carefully. RAW photos: 20–45 MB each depending on camera. A 1-day professional shoot: 500–2,000 photos = 10–90 GB. 4K video: 4–8 GB per hour (H.264), 20–50 GB per hour (ProRes). A wedding videographer shooting 8 hours at 4K: 30–400 GB of footage. Best practice: 3-2-1 backup rule — 3 copies of data, on 2 different media types, with 1 offsite. For 100 GB of photos: local drive + external drive + cloud = 3 copies total.

Data centre and enterprise scale

Modern data centres operate at petabyte and exabyte scale. Facebook stores approximately 250 billion photos (approximately 100 petabytes). YouTube receives 500 hours of video per minute. The entire printed collection of the US Library of Congress is approximately 10 TB. All human speech ever spoken is estimated at approximately 5 exabytes. Understanding the scale from bytes to zettabytes contextualises both individual file management decisions and the infrastructure requirements of large-scale digital services.

💾 Data size quick reference: 1 KB = 1,024 bytes (binary). 1 MB = 1,048,576 bytes. 1 GB = 1,073,741,824 bytes. 1 TB = 1,099,511,627,776 bytes. Network: 1 Mbps = 0.125 MB/s. Common: 1 GB file at 10 Mbps = 800 seconds. 1 TB drive = 931 GB displayed in Windows. A typical smartphone holds 64–256 GB. Average photo: 3–8 MB (JPEG), 20–40 MB (RAW). These reference points make data size decisions intuitive.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many MB in a GB?
1 GB = 1,024 MB (binary, used by operating systems) or 1,000 MB (decimal, used by storage manufacturers). This discrepancy is why a '1TB' hard drive shows as ~931 GB in Windows.
What is the difference between Megabits and Megabytes?
1 Byte = 8 bits. Internet speeds are measured in Megabits per second (Mbps), while file sizes use Megabytes (MB). So a 100 Mbps connection transfers 12.5 MB per second. This is why download speeds appear lower than your advertised connection speed.
How large is a 4K movie?
A typical 4K movie is 50–100 GB in high-quality Blu-ray format, or 15–25 GB in compressed streaming format (Netflix/Disney+). A 1080p HD movie is roughly 4–10 GB. A standard DVD (480p) holds about 4.7 GB.
What is a Petabyte?
1 Petabyte = 1,024 Terabytes = 1,048,576 Gigabytes. To put it in perspective: 1 PB could store approximately 250,000 HD movies or 20 million 4-drawer filing cabinets full of text.
