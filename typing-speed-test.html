Frequently Asked Questions

What is WPM?
WPM (Words Per Minute) is the standard measure of typing speed. It's calculated as (characters typed ÷ 5) ÷ minutes elapsed. Dividing by 5 accounts for the average word length in English. A score of 40 WPM is average, 60 WPM is good, and 80+ WPM is excellent.
What is a good typing speed?
Average adult typing speed is 40 WPM. Office workers typically type 50–70 WPM. Professional typists reach 75–90 WPM. The world record is over 200 WPM. For most jobs, 60+ WPM is sufficient. Programmers average 60–80 WPM but often type in bursts.
How do I improve my typing speed?
Touch typing — using all 10 fingers without looking at the keyboard — is the most effective method. Start slow and focus on accuracy; speed follows naturally. Practice daily for 10–15 minutes. Home row positioning (ASDF JKL;) is the foundation of fast, accurate typing.
Why does my accuracy matter more than speed?
Accuracy reduces errors that need correction. At 95% accuracy, you're only retyping 1 in 20 characters. At 80% accuracy, you're correcting 1 in 5. Consistent 95%+ accuracy at moderate speed is more productive than high speed with many errors.
Is my typing data sent anywhere?
No. The typing test runs entirely in your browser. No keystrokes, text, or results are stored or transmitted anywhere.
