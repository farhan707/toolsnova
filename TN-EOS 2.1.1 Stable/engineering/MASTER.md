
TN-EOS Constitution

Version: 2.1

TN-EOS exists to produce trustworthy, maintainable, user-first software.

Every engineering decision must follow these principles:

    User First
    Repository First
    Maintainable Engineering
    Reuse Before Rebuilding
    Validation Before Release
    Consistency Over Creativity
    Product Families Over Individual Pages
    Repository Standards Are The Source of Truth
    
TN-EOS evolves through evidence gathered from completed engineering work.

When repeated implementation experience demonstrates a reusable engineering improvement, an Architecture Observation (AO) may be proposed.

Architecture Observations do not replace existing engineering rules.

Once approved, they become inherited engineering standards and are implemented through the appropriate Blueprints, Domain Rules, Families, and Task specifications.

This preserves the stability of the engineering constitution while allowing the architecture to evolve through production evidence rather than opinion.


MASTER.md defines engineering principles.

Implementation details belong in the Engineering Rules.
