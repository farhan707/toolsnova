📋 Prompt
You are a social media strategist who has grown B2B and B2C brands from 0 to 100K+ followers.

Brand: [brand name]
Audience: [target audience]
Platforms: [platforms — LinkedIn/Instagram/Twitter-X/TikTok/YouTube]
Goal: [business goal — brand awareness/leads/sales/community]

Task: Build a complete social media content strategy:

1. PLATFORM STRATEGY:
For each platform: Role, content format, posting frequency, success metric

2. CONTENT PILLARS (4–5):
Each pillar: Theme + Why it serves the audience + % of content mix

3. CONTENT FORMATS:
Per platform: Which formats (video/carousel/text/image/poll) and why

4. TONE OF VOICE GUIDE:
3 adjectives + 3 anti-adjectives + 5 sample phrases

5. 30-DAY CONTENT CALENDAR:
Week-by-week plan with 3–5 specific post ideas per week

6. METRICS FRAMEWORK:
What to measure weekly/monthly and what action each metric triggers

💡 Pro Tips
Pick 1–2 platforms and do them well — spreading across 5 platforms with thin content loses to owning 1 platform with deep engagement
Your content pillars should reflect what your audience actually wants to share — not just what you want to say
The 80/20 rule: 80% valuable/educational, 20% promotional. Most brands do this backwards.
Batch create content on one day per week — daily creation produces inconsistent quality; batching lets you be strategic
⚠️ Common Mistakes
Posting without a distribution strategy — publishing and hoping is not a strategy
Measuring vanity metrics (followers) instead of outcome metrics (website visits, leads)
Using the same content format on every platform — a LinkedIn carousel and an Instagram carousel perform completely differently
Ignoring the comment section — replies and engagement in comments amplify reach significantly on every platform
❓ FAQ
  • Which platform should I prioritise in 2026?
    It depends on your audience. B2B and professional audiences: LinkedIn and Twitter/X. B2C and visual brands: Instagram and TikTok. Developer and tech communities: Twitter/X and Reddit. YouTube is platform-agnostic — long-form video builds deep authority for any audience.
  • How long does it take to grow a social media presence?
    For organic growth with consistent, quality content: 6–12 months to see meaningful traction. The compound effect kicks in around month 9–12 as your content catalogue grows and the algorithm learns your audience. There's no shortcut that doesn't eventually cost you.
  • Should I use AI to create all my social content?
    AI is excellent for generating options, drafting captions, and scaling content production. But your best-performing content will be the posts with your genuine take, your specific data, or your real story. Use AI to produce more volume; invest your own thinking in the highest-value content.
  • When should I hire a social media manager?
    When content creation is consistently taking more than 8 hours per week or when you're generating enough revenue that your time is worth more spent elsewhere. Until then, the founder's voice almost always outperforms a hired voice for brand-building.
