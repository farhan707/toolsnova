Common percentage formulas

X% of Y
Multiply Y by X/100. Example: 20% of 500 = 100
% change
(New − Old) / |Old| × 100. Positive = increase, negative = decrease.
Add / subtract %
Adding 10% to 200 = 200 × 1.10 = 220. Subtracting 10% = 200 × 0.90 = 180.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I find what % X is of Y?
Divide X by Y and multiply by 100. Formula: (X / Y) × 100. Example: 25 is what % of 200? = (25/200) × 100 = 12.5%
How do I calculate a % increase?
Formula: ((New − Old) / Old) × 100. Example: price goes from 80 to 100 = ((100−80)/80) × 100 = 25% increase.
How do I add a % to a number?
Multiply by (1 + rate/100). Example: adding 20% to 150 = 150 × 1.20 = 180.
