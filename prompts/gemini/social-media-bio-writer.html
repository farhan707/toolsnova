📋 Prompt
You are a personal branding expert who has written social media bios for creators with 1M+ followers and executives at top companies.

Your name/brand: [name or brand]
What you do: [what you do]
Who you help or serve: [who you help or serve]
Best result or credential: [your best result or credential]
Personality: [personality — serious/playful/minimal/bold/professional]

Task: Write platform-optimised bios for each platform:

INSTAGRAM (150 chars max):
- Line 1: What you do (hook)
- Line 2: Who you help or proof point
- Line 3: CTA with link direction
- Emoji usage: match personality

TWITTER/X (160 chars max):
- One sentence that captures your value
- Optional: personality signal
- No wasted words

LINKEDIN HEADLINE (220 chars max):
- Value proposition | Who you help | Proof point
- Keywords recruiters/clients search

TIKTOK (80 chars visible before 'more'):
- Instant hook in first 5 words
- What to expect from your content

YOUTUBE CHANNEL DESCRIPTION (first 125 chars visible):
- Channel value proposition
- Upload schedule
- Subscribe CTA

For each: provide 2 variations (bold/safe), character count, and platform-specific optimisation note

💡 Pro Tips
Your Instagram bio should work without the link in bio — don't make the entire value proposition dependent on clicking away
LinkedIn headline is indexed by LinkedIn's internal search — include the keywords your target audience would search ('SEO consultant' not 'growth wizard')
TikTok shows only the first 80 characters without clicking 'more' — your hook must be complete in that space
Twitter/X bios appear in search results — include your key topic keywords naturally
⚠️ Common Mistakes
Same bio on every platform — Instagram and LinkedIn audiences behave differently; your bio should too
No CTA — every bio should give people somewhere to go or something to do
Listing your job titles instead of the value you create — 'Content Manager' is a title; 'I help B2B companies get 3× more from their content team' is a value proposition
Emojis that don't add meaning — decorative emojis look unprofessional; emojis that replace or emphasise words add value
❓ FAQ
  • Should my bio be identical on all platforms?
    No. The core message should be consistent (same value proposition), but the format, tone, and content should be native to each platform. LinkedIn readers are professional; TikTok readers are casual. Instagram readers are visual; Twitter/X readers are text-native. Adapt accordingly.
  • How often should I update my social bio?
    When your role, main offer, or most impressive credential changes. A bio that mentions a 2020 achievement in 2026 signals you haven't evolved. LinkedIn specifically — update your headline whenever your position, focus, or audience changes significantly.
  • Should I include keywords in my bio?
    Yes, especially on LinkedIn and Twitter/X — both platforms use bio keywords in search. Instagram's search is primarily hashtag and location-based, though account name and bio keywords matter. TikTok's search increasingly uses bio content.
  • Is Claude better than ChatGPT for bio writing?
    Both are effective for bio writing. Claude is stronger at following strict character count constraints and maintaining consistent voice across variations. ChatGPT is slightly faster at generating many options quickly. Specify exact character limits in your prompt for best results.
