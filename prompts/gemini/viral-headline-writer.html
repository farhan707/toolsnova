📋 Prompt
You are a headline specialist who writes viral titles for major blogs, YouTube channels, and email campaigns.

Topic: [content topic]
Platform: [target platform — blog/YouTube/email/LinkedIn/Twitter]
Audience: [audience]
Emotion: [primary emotion — curiosity/surprise/desire/fear/joy]

Task: Write 20 headline variations across 5 frameworks:

FRAMEWORK 1 — NUMBER LIST (4 variants): '[N] [adjective] ways to [outcome]'
FRAMEWORK 2 — HOW TO (4 variants): 'How to [achieve X] without [sacrifice Y]'
FRAMEWORK 3 — QUESTION (4 variants): Creates an information gap the reader must close
FRAMEWORK 4 — BOLD STATEMENT (4 variants): Counterintuitive claim + proof element
FRAMEWORK 5 — STORY HOOK (4 variants): '[I/they] [did surprising thing]. Here's what happened.'

For each headline: [headline] — [why this works psychologically]
Finish with: Top pick for [platform] + recommended A/B test pair
📄 Example output
TOPIC: AI prompts for SEO | PLATFORM: Blog | EMOTION: Curiosity

NUMBER LIST:
'7 ChatGPT Prompts That Write Better SEO Articles Than Most Copywriters' — specificity + implicit comparison creates intrigue
'3 AI Prompts That Doubled My Blog Traffic in 30 Days' — small number = achievable, personal proof = credible
'15 Free SEO Prompts Every Content Writer Should Save' — 'save' signals high value, 'free' removes barrier
'50 Tested AI Prompts Ranked by Traffic Impact' — large number + ranked = definitive resource

HOW TO:
'How to Write SEO Articles 10× Faster Without Sacrificing Quality' — speed + quality trade-off resolved
'How I Get ChatGPT to Write Content Google Actually Ranks' — personal + specific outcome + implied competitor failure

STORY HOOK:
'I Tested 50 AI Prompts on One Article. Only 3 Actually Ranked.' — experiment + specific failure rate = must know which 3
'My SEO Traffic Doubled After Changing One Thing in My ChatGPT Prompts' — transformation + single change = accessible

TOP PICK: '7 ChatGPT Prompts That Write Better SEO Articles Than Most Copywriters'
A/B TEST vs: 'How I Get ChatGPT to Write Content Google Actually Ranks'
Hypothesis: Number list wins on scan, story hook wins on emotional engagement. Test on your specific audience.

💡 Pro Tips
Numbers make headlines more specific and scannable — odd numbers (7, 11, 23) outperform even numbers in CTR studies
The curiosity gap ('here's what happened') works best when the outcome is genuinely surprising
Match headline length to platform: Twitter under 60 chars, SEO titles 50–60 chars, email subject under 50 chars
Test 2 headlines per major piece — after 20 examples you'll know your audience's preference pattern
⚠️ Common Mistakes
Clickbait that under-delivers on the content — accurately compelling beats dishonestly enticing
Vague benefit words ('better', 'amazing') — 'doubled traffic' beats 'improved traffic' every time
Generic headlines that 100 other articles use for the same keyword
Writing headlines for yourself instead of for your specific audience's language
❓ FAQ
  • What's the ideal headline length?
    For SEO: 50–60 characters to avoid SERP truncation. For email subject lines: under 50 characters for mobile. For social: whatever the platform allows while remaining punchy. Test length variations — longer headlines sometimes win for complex topics.
  • Does the headline really matter more than the content?
    For social and email, yes — 80% of people read the headline, 20% read the rest. For SEO long-term, content quality dominates. The practical answer: a great headline gets the content seen; great content keeps readers and earns rankings.
  • Should I optimise headlines for CTR or search ranking?
    Both simultaneously is the goal. Include the target keyword (ranking signal) AND a compelling hook (CTR signal). Higher CTR is also an indirect ranking signal for Google — pages that get clicked more rank higher over time.
  • Can AI generate genuinely viral headlines?
    AI generates technically correct variations of proven headline formulas. True virality often requires human cultural timing and genuine surprise. Use AI for options; human judgement for selection.
