📋 Prompt
You are a conversion copywriter and SEO specialist who creates FAQ pages that both rank in Google and convert visitors into customers.

Product/Service: [product or service]
Customer: [target customer]
Objections: [main objections — price/trust/time/complexity]
Common questions: [common questions from your customers]

Task: Write a complete FAQ page (15–20 questions) organised into sections:

SECTION 1 — BASICS (5 questions): What it is, who it's for, how it works
SECTION 2 — PRICING (3 questions): Cost, what's included, payment
SECTION 3 — HOW TO GET STARTED (3 questions): Setup, timeline, what you need
SECTION 4 — COMMON CONCERNS (4 questions): The objections people have but don't say
SECTION 5 — ADVANCED (3 questions): For users who are already engaged

FOR EACH Q&A:
- Question: Phrase it exactly as a customer would say it
- Answer: 40–100 words. Specific, honest. Convert objections into advantages where genuine.

ALSO INCLUDE:
- FAQPage Schema JSON-LD (ready to paste)
- Recommended placement on the page

💡 Pro Tips
Phrase questions exactly as users type them — this is how you capture featured snippets and 'People Also Ask'
Answer the question in the first sentence, then expand — people scan FAQ pages
Include the most emotionally charged objections (price, trust, time) even if uncomfortable — avoiding them doesn't make them disappear
Add FAQPage schema to every FAQ page — it's the easiest structured data to implement and delivers featured snippet eligibility
⚠️ Common Mistakes
Writing questions no one actually asks ('Can I integrate with your API?') when the real question is 'Is this worth paying for?'
Answers over 150 words — if an FAQ answer is that long, it should be a dedicated page
Missing the pricing section — 'How much does it cost?' is always in the top 3 questions for any paid product
Generic answers that don't actually answer the question ('We take security very seriously') — say specifically what you do
❓ FAQ
  • How many FAQ questions should I include?
    10–20 is the sweet spot. Below 8, you look like you're avoiding questions. Above 25, the page becomes hard to navigate. Use a sticky sidebar or jump links for pages with 15+ questions.
  • Should FAQ answers be short or detailed?
    40–100 words per answer for most questions. Longer for complex technical questions. The goal is to fully answer the question with zero unnecessary words.
  • How does FAQ content help SEO?
    FAQ pages capture long-tail question queries ('How do I...'), appear in 'People Also Ask' boxes, and can earn featured snippets. FAQPage schema markup makes you eligible for rich results in Google search. This is one of the highest-ROI content types for organic search.
  • Should I put the FAQ on its own page or embed it on product pages?
    Both. A dedicated FAQ page helps SEO and provides a place to send customers with complex questions. Embedding a short FAQ section (5–7 questions) on each product/service page directly addresses objections at the point of purchase.
