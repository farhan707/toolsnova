📋 Prompt
You are a B2B content marketer who writes case studies that close enterprise deals.

Customer: [customer name — or use 'the client' if confidential]
Problem: [problem they had]
Solution: [solution you provided]
Results: [measurable results — % improvement, time saved, money generated]

Task: Write a complete customer case study:

1. HEADLINE: Result-first. '[Customer] [Result] with [Solution]' format
2. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY: 3 sentences. Problem → Solution → Result. One key stat.
3. THE CHALLENGE: Before state. What was broken, what it was costing them, why they needed to act.
4. WHY THEY CHOSE US: What differentiated you vs. alternatives they considered
5. THE SOLUTION: Specific implementation details (not generic — show the work)
6. THE RESULTS: Primary metric. Secondary metrics. Qualitative outcome.
7. QUOTE: From the decision-maker — specific, result-focused, quotable
8. WHAT'S NEXT: What they're doing next with your product

Format: Subheadings, short paragraphs, pull-quote box, data highlights in bold.
Length: 600–900 words.
📄 Example output
**DataStack Reduces Customer Onboarding Time by 72% with ToolsNova Workflow Templates**

**EXECUTIVE SUMMARY**
DataStack, a B2B analytics platform serving 800+ enterprise clients, was losing 34% of new users in the first week. After implementing ToolsNova's AI-assisted onboarding templates, they reduced median time-to-value from 11 days to 3.1 days and cut first-week churn by 72% in 90 days.

**THE CHALLENGE**
DataStack's core product was powerful — but complex. New enterprise customers arrived with high expectations and immediately hit a wall: a 47-step setup process, 3 required integrations, and a data model that needed to be configured from scratch for every client...

**THE RESULTS**
• Time-to-first-value: 11 days → 3.1 days (72% reduction)
• Week-1 churn: 34% → 9.4% (72% reduction)
• Customer Success team capacity freed: 40%

> 'We'd been trying to solve this with headcount for two years. ToolsNova solved it in 6 weeks. The ROI calculation took about 4 minutes.' — [Name], VP Customer Success, DataStack

💡 Pro Tips
Lead with the result in the headline — 'How DataStack Solved Onboarding' is a process story; 'DataStack Cuts Onboarding by 72%' is a result story
Get the quote before you write the case study — the quote often contains the best angle for the whole piece
Use before/after numbers in bold wherever possible — they're the most scanned element
Ask the customer what they'd tell a peer considering your product — this is often more compelling than your own framing
⚠️ Common Mistakes
Generic before-states ('they had a problem with efficiency') — get specific about what the problem was actually costing
Over-focusing on your product's features instead of the customer's transformation
Not including the ROI calculation — enterprise buyers need to justify the purchase internally
Missing the 'why you' section — this is where you differentiate from alternatives the buyer considered
❓ FAQ
  • How do I get customers to agree to a case study?
    Make it easy and valuable for them: offer to write the draft for their review, give them final approval rights, and offer to promote it to your audience (they get coverage too). Timing matters: ask 60–90 days after a clear success, not immediately.
  • What if the customer won't allow their name to be used?
    Anonymous case studies still work. Use '[Leading US Retailer]' or 'A Fortune 500 logistics company'. Include enough context to be credible (size, industry, challenge) and focus heavily on the specific results.
  • How long should a case study be?
    600–900 words for a standard case study. The executive summary (3 sentences) is the most important section — many readers go no further. A one-page PDF version for sales teams is often more useful than the web version.
  • Should I use Claude or ChatGPT for case studies?
    Claude is stronger for case studies — it writes with more strategic framing, follows complex structural requirements more faithfully, and produces tighter, more professional prose. Provide as many specific numbers as you have.
