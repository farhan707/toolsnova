How to Use the Currency Converter

  1. Enter the amount — the sum you want to convert.
  2. Select source currency — the currency you are converting from.
  3. Select target currency — the currency you are converting to.
  4. Read the converted amount — the equivalent value in the target currency at the current exchange rate.
  5. Check the rate — note the exchange rate used. Compare this to rates offered by banks and exchange services to identify the most favourable option for large transfers.
💱 Rate comparison tip: The rate shown is the mid-market (interbank) rate — the 'real' rate between currencies. Banks and exchange services add a margin of 1–5% on top. For large transfers, specialist services like Wise (TransferWise) or Revolut often offer rates 2–4% better than traditional banks.

Understanding Exchange Rates

📊 Mid-Market Rate
The midpoint between buy and sell rates — the 'real' exchange rate used between banks. This is the rate shown on financial websites and this calculator. Retail customers never receive this rate — providers add a margin.
💱 Bid-Ask Spread
Banks and exchange services buy currency at the 'bid' rate (lower) and sell at the 'ask' rate (higher). The spread between these is their profit. A 2% spread on a $10,000 transfer means $200 goes to the provider.
📈 Factors Affecting Rates
Interest rate differentials between countries, inflation rates, political stability, trade balances, and market sentiment all influence exchange rates. Major economic announcements can move rates by 1–3% in minutes.
⏰ Rate Volatility
Exchange rates fluctuate continuously during market hours. The forex market operates 24/5. Major currency pairs (EURUSD, GBPUSD) typically move 0.5–1% daily. Exotic pairs can move 2–5% on volatile days.
🌍 Fixed vs Floating Rates
Most major currencies float freely against each other. Some countries peg their currency to another (Saudi Riyal to USD at fixed rate). Pegged currencies offer rate stability but require the country to maintain large foreign exchange reserves.
💳 Real Cost of Currency Exchange
Total cost = Exchange rate margin + transfer fees. A bank charging 3% margin + $15 fee on a $500 transfer costs $30 — 6% total. A specialist service charging 0.5% with no fee costs $2.50. For frequent transfers, the difference is substantial.

Currency Exchange for Travel and Business

Best ways to exchange currency

From best to worst value: 1) Specialist transfer services (Wise, Revolut) — closest to mid-market rate for transfers. 2) ATM withdrawals abroad — your bank's rate plus ATM fee, but usually better than exchange bureaus. 3) Credit cards with no foreign transaction fee — typically good rates automatically. 4) Airport exchange bureaus — worst rates, typically 8–12% worse than mid-market. 5) Hotel exchanges — similar to airport, avoid for large amounts.

Business currency risk

Businesses with international revenues or expenses face currency risk — the risk that exchange rate movements reduce profits. A UK exporter quoting in USD faces loss if GBP strengthens between quote and payment. Hedging tools like forward contracts (locking in today's rate for future delivery) and currency options protect against adverse rate movements.

Dollar-cost averaging for currency

For large regular currency transfers (e.g. monthly salary in foreign currency, regular overseas payments), transferring fixed amounts at regular intervals averages out exchange rate fluctuations over time. This is equivalent to DCA investing — not trying to time the market, but achieving a reasonable average rate consistently.

💱 Transfer checklist: 1) Compare mid-market rate to offered rate — the difference is your cost. 2) Add all fees to calculate total cost. 3) For amounts above $1,000, compare at least 3 providers. 4) For recurring transfers, set up a rate alert to transfer when rates are favourable. 5) For large transfers ($10,000+), consider forward contracts to lock in favourable rates.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are the exchange rates live?
The rates in this tool are indicative reference rates updated periodically. For real-time live rates, use a bank, forex broker, or services like Google Finance or XE.com. Exchange rates fluctuate constantly during market hours.
What is the difference between buy and sell rates?
Banks offer two rates: the buy rate (what they pay you when you sell foreign currency) and the sell rate (what they charge you when you buy foreign currency). The difference is the spread — the bank's profit margin. This tool shows mid-market rates between buy and sell.
Why is my bank rate different from this calculator?
Banks add a margin (spread) of 1–5% on top of the mid-market rate. Money changers and forex brokers may offer better rates. For large transfers, compare multiple services — even 0.5% difference on $10,000 is $50.
What is USD/PKR exchange rate?
The USD to PKR rate fluctuates based on Pakistan's foreign exchange market, State Bank of Pakistan interventions, and global dollar strength. Check the State Bank of Pakistan website or a forex broker for the current interbank rate.
