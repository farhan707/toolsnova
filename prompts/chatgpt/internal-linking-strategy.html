📋 Prompt
You are an SEO architect who uses internal linking to dramatically improve ranking distribution across a site.

Main topic: [main topic]
Existing pages: [number of existing pages]
Priority pages: [top priority pages — list 3–5 most important]
Site type: [site type — blog/tool site/e-commerce/SaaS]

Task: Build a complete internal linking strategy:

1. LINK EQUITY ANALYSIS:
How link equity currently flows (based on site type) and where it should flow

2. PRIORITY PAGE PROTECTION:
For each of your [top priority pages]: Which 5 pages should link to it and with what anchor text

3. HUB & SPOKE ARCHITECTURE:
How to restructure existing content into hub-and-spoke for topical authority

4. ANCHOR TEXT GUIDELINES:
Branded vs. exact match vs. partial match distribution

5. ORPHAN PAGE RESCUE:
How to identify orphan pages and integrate them into your architecture

6. IMPLEMENTATION CHECKLIST:
Specific actions in priority order with effort estimate for each

💡 Pro Tips
Every new page you publish should link to at least 3 existing pages — don't create orphans
Your homepage's link equity is your most valuable asset — be intentional about where it points
The anchor text of internal links is a ranking signal — but over-optimising exact-match anchor text looks unnatural
Use your site search data: queries with no results show you what content is missing and what internal links would help users find
⚠️ Common Mistakes
Linking to everything from everything — dilutes link equity and creates confusing site architecture
Using 'click here' or 'read more' as anchor text — these pass no topical context
Ignoring existing content when publishing new pages — every new publish is an opportunity to add links from relevant existing pages
Building a hierarchy that's too deep — important pages shouldn't be more than 3 clicks from the homepage
❓ FAQ
  • How many internal links per page is ideal?
    There's no fixed number, but for most pages 5–15 internal links is appropriate. Contextual links within body content carry more value than navigation or footer links. Don't add internal links just to hit a number — relevance is the key criterion.
  • Does internal linking help new pages rank faster?
    Yes. New pages with no internal links pointing to them (orphan pages) are rarely crawled, rarely indexed quickly, and receive no link equity. A new page with 5–10 relevant internal links from existing high-traffic pages indexes and ranks significantly faster.
  • Should I use exact match keywords as anchor text for internal links?
    Occasionally, yes — for your most important target pages. But the distribution should be varied: branded anchor text, partial match, and natural phrases should dominate. A page where every internal link uses exact-match keywords looks manipulative to Google.
  • How do I find orphan pages on my site?
    Crawl your site with Screaming Frog (free up to 500 URLs) and compare the crawled URL list against your sitemap. Any URLs in the sitemap not found by the crawler (because no page links to them) are orphans. Google Search Console Coverage report also shows crawled vs. indexed discrepancies.
