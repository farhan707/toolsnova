📋 Prompt
You are an SEO analyst who has grown organic traffic from zero to 500,000 monthly visitors through competitive research.

Your site: [your website or description]
Keyword space: [target keyword topic]
Top competitors: [top 3 competitor URLs]
Current situation: [new/existing site with X organic visitors]

Task:
1. COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE: How to assess domain authority, estimated traffic, keyword focus
2. KEYWORD GAP: How to find keywords competitors rank for that you don't; Priority: Volume × Intent × Difficulty; Top 10 highest-opportunity keywords
3. CONTENT GAP: Types competitors have that you lack; 5 content pieces to create first; Formats they're not using
4. BACKLINK GAP: Sources linking to competitors not you; 5 high-priority opportunities with outreach angles
5. TECHNICAL COMPARISON: Core Web Vitals approach, schema gaps
6. 90-DAY PLAN: Weeks 1–4 quick wins, weeks 5–8 content, weeks 9–12 links
7. TRACKING: Weekly metrics + free and paid tools

💡 Pro Tips
Target keywords where all 3 competitors rank — proven demand
Content gaps close faster than backlink gaps — create missing content first
Keywords where competitors rank #4–10 are ripe — a better page can displace them
Use free tools first: Google Search Console, Ubersuggest free, Ahrefs free checker
⚠️ Common Mistakes
Analysing domain authority instead of page-level competition — you compete page-to-page
Targeting keywords without matching competitor content depth
Ignoring Core Web Vitals — slow pages undermine even excellent content
One-time analysis — competitors evolve; refresh quarterly
❓ FAQ
  • Free tools for SEO competitor analysis?
    Google Search Console (your data), Ubersuggest free tier, Ahrefs free backlink checker, Google PageSpeed Insights. For deeper analysis: Semrush 7-day trial or Ahrefs limited free plan.
  • How long to outrank a competitor?
    Low-competition: 3–6 months. Medium: 6–12 months. High-competition established pages: 12–24 months. Domain authority and backlinks significantly affect the timeline.
  • Target all 3 or just the weakest?
    Start where your weakest competitor ranks — easiest to displace. Move up once you've won those positions.
  • Best model for SEO analysis?
    Claude — maintains logical consistency across complex multi-part analysis and accurately identifies SEO strategy implications. Provide real URLs and metrics for best results.
