How to Use the Ovulation Calculator

  1. Enter first day of last period — the first day of bleeding of your most recent menstrual period.
  2. Select cycle length — the number of days from the first day of one period to the first day of the next. Average is 28 days but normal range is 21–35 days.
  3. Read your fertile window — the 6-day window including the 5 days before ovulation and ovulation day itself.
  4. Note peak ovulation day — marked with ⭐. This is the highest-fertility day when the egg is released.
  5. View upcoming cycles — the calculator shows fertile windows for your next 3 cycles for advance planning.
⭐ The fertile window: Pregnancy is only possible during a 6-day window per cycle — the 5 days before ovulation and ovulation day itself. Sperm survive up to 5 days in the reproductive tract, while an egg survives only 12–24 hours after release. The highest conception probability is from 2 days before to 1 day after ovulation.

Understanding the Menstrual Cycle

📅 Cycle Phases
Follicular phase (days 1–14): menstruation then follicle development, estrogen rises. Ovulation (around day 14): LH surge triggers egg release. Luteal phase (days 15–28): progesterone rises, preparing uterus for implantation. If no fertilisation, progesterone drops and menstruation begins.
🥚 Ovulation Timing
Ovulation occurs approximately 14 days BEFORE the next period — not 14 days after the last period (which is only accurate for 28-day cycles). For a 35-day cycle, ovulation is around day 21. For a 21-day cycle, ovulation is around day 7. Cycle length varies; ovulation day varies accordingly.
📊 Cycle Variability
Most women have cycle-to-cycle variation of 2–4 days even with 'regular' cycles. Stress, illness, travel, significant weight changes, and intensive exercise can delay ovulation. The luteal phase (after ovulation) is relatively fixed at 12–16 days; the follicular phase (before ovulation) is where most variability occurs.
🌡️ BBT Tracking
Basal body temperature rises 0.2–0.5°C after ovulation due to progesterone. Tracking BBT daily (same time, before getting up) confirms ovulation occurred and helps identify your personal ovulation pattern over multiple cycles. BBT confirms past ovulation — it cannot predict upcoming ovulation.
💊 OPK Tests
Ovulation predictor kits detect the LH surge that occurs 24–36 hours before ovulation. More predictive than calendar methods. Digital OPKs show high fertility (rising estrogen) and peak fertility (LH surge). Most useful for cycle lengths that are irregular or significantly different from 28 days.
🔬 Factors Affecting Ovulation
Regular ovulation can be disrupted by: PCOS (polycystic ovary syndrome), thyroid disorders, significant weight loss or gain, excessive exercise, high stress, hormonal contraceptives (fertility typically returns within 1–3 months after stopping). Irregular cycles warrant medical evaluation.

Conception and Cycle Tracking

Maximising conception chances

The most evidence-based approach: have intercourse every 1–2 days throughout the fertile window rather than trying to pinpoint the exact ovulation day. This approach is less stressful, accounts for ovulation prediction uncertainty, and ensures sperm are present when the egg is released. For couples trying to conceive, daily or every-other-day intercourse from cycle day 10 to day 18 (for a 28-day cycle) covers the fertile window regardless of exact ovulation timing.

When to see a doctor

Seek medical evaluation if: no pregnancy after 12 months of regular unprotected intercourse (6 months if over 35), cycles are consistently irregular (shorter than 21 days or longer than 35), periods are absent (more than 3 months without pregnancy), or known fertility concerns exist. Early evaluation identifies treatable conditions — the majority of fertility issues have effective medical solutions.

Cycle tracking apps

Apps like Clue, Flo, Natural Cycles, and Kindara combine calendar prediction with BBT and symptom tracking for more accurate ovulation prediction. After 3–6 months of data, apps learn individual cycle patterns and provide increasingly personalised predictions. No app replaces OPK tests or medical evaluation for clinical accuracy, but they provide excellent day-to-day cycle awareness.

📅 For conception planning: Track 3–6 cycles before trying to conceive to understand your personal cycle length and ovulation timing. This data makes this calculator's predictions significantly more accurate for your individual pattern. Record cycle length monthly and use the average for best results.

Frequently Asked Questions

When do I ovulate?
Ovulation typically occurs 14 days before your next period, regardless of cycle length. For a 28-day cycle: day 14. For a 32-day cycle: day 18. The fertile window is 5 days before ovulation plus the day of ovulation itself.
What is the fertile window?
The fertile window is the 6 days during which pregnancy is possible: the 5 days before ovulation and the day of ovulation itself. Sperm can survive up to 5 days in the reproductive tract, while an egg survives 12-24 hours after release.
How accurate is this calculator?
This calculator assumes regular cycles. Irregular cycles, stress, illness, PCOS, and other factors affect ovulation timing. For accurate tracking, use basal body temperature (BBT) monitoring or ovulation predictor kits (OPKs) alongside this calculator.
