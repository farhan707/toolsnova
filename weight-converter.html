Understanding Weight and Mass Units

⚖️ Metric Units
Gram (g): base unit, mass of 1 cm³ of water at 4°C. Kilogram (kg) = 1,000 g: SI base unit of mass, everyday body weight and food. Tonne/Metric ton = 1,000 kg: industrial and commercial weights. Milligram (mg) = 0.001 g: medication dosages, small measurements. Microgram (μg) = 0.000001 g: trace elements, pharmacology.
⚖️ Imperial/US Units
Ounce (oz) = 28.3495 g. Pound (lb) = 16 oz = 453.592 g. Stone = 14 lbs = 6.35 kg (UK body weight). US Ton (short ton) = 2,000 lbs = 907.185 kg. UK Ton (long ton) = 2,240 lbs = 1,016.05 kg. Metric Tonne = 1,000 kg = 2,204.62 lbs. The three 'ton' definitions cause constant confusion in international shipping.
🌍 Weight in Daily Life
Body weight: kg in most countries, stones and pounds in UK, pounds in US. Cooking: grams and kg in metric countries, cups/ounces in US. Luggage: airline limits in kg globally (even US airlines use kg internationally). Shipping: typically kg metric, though US domestic shipping uses pounds. Jewellery: troy ounce (31.1 g) for precious metals, different from avoirdupois ounce (28.3 g).
💊 Medical Weight
Drug dosages are calculated by body weight in mg/kg. A medication prescribed at 10 mg/kg for a 70 kg patient = 700 mg dose. Paediatric dosing is particularly weight-dependent — accurate weight conversion is clinically critical. IV fluid rates are also weight-based. Medical records use kg globally even in US hospitals (though patients often know their weight in lbs).
🏋️ The Stone (UK)
14 pounds = 1 stone — a uniquely British unit for body weight. A person weighing 11 stone 6 lbs weighs 160 lbs = 72.6 kg. Many British people know their weight in stones and pounds but not kg. The unit persists culturally despite metric adoption because changing how people express body weight requires cultural not just official change. Bathroom scales in the UK often show all three: kg, stone/lb, and lb.
⚗️ Mass vs Weight
Mass: amount of matter, measured in kg, constant everywhere. Weight: gravitational force on mass, measured in Newtons, varies with gravity. On the Moon (1/6 Earth gravity): your mass is 70 kg but you weigh only 115 N (vs 686 N on Earth). In everyday speech, 'weight' means mass — scales measure force but are calibrated in mass units assuming Earth gravity.

Weight Conversion in Real Applications

International shipping and customs

Shipping weights affect costs and customs declarations. Most international carriers (DHL, FedEx, UPS) use kg. Dimensional weight (volumetric weight) = length × width × height (cm) ÷ 5,000 for international air — the greater of actual weight or dimensional weight is charged. US domestic shipping uses pounds. Customs declarations require accurate weight in the importing country's preferred unit. Overweight airline baggage fees are calculated per kg over the limit.

Cooking weight conversions

US recipes use cups and ounces; most international recipes use grams. Volume-to-weight conversion depends on ingredient density: 1 cup flour ≈ 120–130g (varies by sifting and packing). 1 cup sugar = 200g. 1 cup butter = 227g. 1 cup water = 237ml = 237g. Kitchen scales using grams produce more consistent results than volume measurements because ingredient density varies. Professional baking recipes worldwide use gram measurements — the global industry standard for precision.

Fitness and nutrition tracking

Food nutrition labels show values per 100g (international standard) or per serving (varies). Protein targets are expressed in g/kg of bodyweight (1.6–2.2 g/kg for muscle building). Calorie tracking apps allow unit selection but typically store in grams internally. Converting between US nutrition labels (ounces) and international standards (grams) is a constant need for anyone following plans from different countries. The universal language of nutrition is grams per kilogram of bodyweight.

⚖️ Weight conversion quick reference: 1 kg = 2.205 lbs. 1 lb = 453.6 g = 0.4536 kg. 1 oz = 28.35 g. 1 stone = 6.35 kg = 14 lbs. 1 metric tonne = 1,000 kg = 2,205 lbs. Common body weights: 50 kg = 110 lbs. 60 kg = 132 lbs. 70 kg = 154 lbs. 80 kg = 176 lbs. 90 kg = 198 lbs. 100 kg = 220 lbs. Memorising a few reference points makes estimation fast.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many pounds in a kilogram?
1 kilogram = 2.20462 pounds. Conversely, 1 pound = 0.453592 kg. Quick estimate: multiply kg by 2.2 to get pounds.
How do I convert kg to stones and pounds?
1 stone = 14 pounds = 6.35029 kg. To convert kg: divide by 6.35029 to get stones, then multiply the decimal part by 14 for extra pounds. Example: 75 kg = 11.81 stone = 11 stone 11.4 lbs.
What is the difference between mass and weight?
Mass is the amount of matter in an object (measured in kg). Weight is the force of gravity on that mass (measured in Newtons). In everyday usage, weight and mass are used interchangeably, but scientifically 1 kg of mass weighs about 9.81 Newtons on Earth.
How many grams in an ounce?
1 ounce (oz) = 28.3495 grams. 1 pound = 16 ounces = 453.592 grams. In cooking, recipes often use both metric (grams) and imperial (ounces) measurements.
