📋 Prompt
You are a senior SEO content writer and strategist with 10+ years of experience writing high-ranking blog posts.

Context: Write a complete, publish-ready blog post optimised for search engines and human readers in 2026.

Topic: [topic]
Target keyword: [target keyword]
Word count: [word count] words
Audience: [audience]

Task: Write the full blog post including:
- A compelling H1 title that includes the target keyword naturally
- A hook introduction (first 2 sentences must create curiosity or share a surprising fact)
- 4–5 H2 sections with comprehensive coverage of the topic
- Bullet points or numbered lists where appropriate
- Real examples and actionable advice in each section
- A conclusion with a clear call to action

Format: Markdown with H1, H2, H3 headers. Bold key terms. Use bullet points for lists.
Tone: Informative, helpful, conversational. No corporate jargon.
Constraints: Do not use filler phrases like 'In conclusion' or 'It is worth noting'. Include the target keyword in the H1, first paragraph, and at least 2 H2 headings naturally. Never keyword-stuff.

# 7 Proven Ways to Improve Your Credit Score in 2026

Last year, 42% of Americans were rejected for a loan they needed — not because of their income, but because of a three-digit number they didn't fully understand. Your credit score is the most important financial metric most people ignore until it's too late.

## What Actually Moves Your Credit Score

Contrary to popular belief, paying your bills on time is only part of the equation...

💡 Pro Tips
Include your target keyword in the first 100 words — this is a strong on-page signal
Use H3 sub-headings inside H2 sections for complex topics to improve readability
Add a 'Key Takeaways' box near the top — people scroll first, then read
Internal link to 3 related pages on your site within the article body
Images with proper alt text improve time-on-page significantly
⚠️ Common Mistakes
Keyword stuffing — Google's 2026 algorithm penalises unnatural keyword density
Writing for search engines instead of readers — the two should be the same
Ignoring search intent — a 'how to' article ranks poorly for commercial intent queries
Publishing without updating — refresh articles every 6–12 months with new data
❓ FAQ
  • Can I use this prompt with Claude?
    Yes. This prompt works excellently with Claude Opus 4 and Sonnet 4. Claude is particularly strong at following complex formatting instructions and producing nuanced long-form content.
  • How long should the article be?
    For most topics, 1,500–2,500 words is the sweet spot in 2026. Match the length of the top-ranking articles for your keyword — more isn't always better.
  • Will Google penalise AI-written content?
    Google's policy targets low-quality, unhelpful content — not AI content specifically. Add your own insights, examples, and expertise before publishing. Human review and editing is still essential.
  • How do I customise this for my niche?
    Replace [topic] with your specific subject, [target keyword] with your primary keyword from your keyword research tool, [word count] with your target length (check competitors), and [audience] with your reader persona.
